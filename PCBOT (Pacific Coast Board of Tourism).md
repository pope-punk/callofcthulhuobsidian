## Overview
The Pacific Coast Board of Tourism, or PCBOT, is ostensibly a governmental body responsible for promoting tourism and coordinating related activities on the West Coast of the United States. In reality, this public-facing identity serves as a carefully constructed cover for its true function: the primary **oversight organization** for the various West Coast [[Mirror-Breaker Organizations]]. However, far from being a neutral arbiter or a simple administrative layer, PCBOT has become a significant player in its own right, deeply enmeshed in the clandestine power struggles, ideological conflicts, and dangerous operations that define the hidden war against [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONINs]] and other infohazardous threats.

## A History of Shifting Power and Ideology
PCBOT was established in the aftermath of the [[GANYMEDE (1979)]] disaster and the subsequent dissolution of the [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]]. Its initial mandate was likely to coordinate the activities of the newly formed, decentralized Mirror-Breaker cells and to liaise with higher governmental bodies, including the [[CIA]].
* **Early Leadership and Factionalism:** The organization was initially led by figures like [[Jacqui Lopez (Director Javelin)|Jacqui Lopez]] (Director Journey/Javelin), who seemingly favored a more traditional adherence to the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol]]'s principles. During her tenure, internal debates shaped key strategic decisions, such as the relocation of the [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)|BNJC]] to [[Sequoia Falls, WA]], to investigate the remnants of [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]] and act as a check on [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA's]] growing influence. [[Dylan Hill (Agent Destination)|Dylan Hill]] was assigned as a PCBOT liaison during this period, reflecting Lopez's operational approach.
* **The Caraway Coup (Late 1994):** A pivotal shift occurred in late 1994 when [[Henry Caraway (Agent Hotel)|Henry Caraway]], an ambitious agent with strong [[CIA]] backing, orchestrated a coup, effectively ousting Lopez and her allies. This power grab was preceded by the assassination of Lopez's key government supporter, [[Robert Toomey]], by the [[CIA]], signaling a decisive move towards a new, more centralized vision for PCBOT.
* **Alignment with BSSA and CIA:** Under Caraway's directorship, PCBOT has become closely aligned with both the [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]] (particularly through [[Katherine Hill]], who holds a dual role in both organizations) and the [[CIA]]. This alliance reflects a shared agenda: the consolidation of all Mirror-Breaker activities under a unified command structure, the dismantling of the original Protocol's emphasis on compartmentalization and metaphorical obscurity, and a preference for more direct, technologically-driven, and often aggressive countermeasures against infohazards.

## Current Operations and Motivations (Late October 1997)
As of late October 1997, PCBOT, under Director Caraway, operates as a paranoid and increasingly desperate entity. The organization's headquarters in Washington D.C. (concealed behind the mundane facade of a tourism agency office) has become a blacksite for sensitive operations.
* **Agent Chrysalis's Captivity:** For over two weeks, Director Caraway has held [[Agent Chrysalis]] captive. Chrysalis, the last surviving field agent of [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]], fled to PCBOT HQ from Sequoia Falls in late September, bringing with him terrifying samples of [[Rhea]]'s [[Spectator Grubs]] and panicked warnings. Caraway, lacking the full context of [[Rhea]]'s existence and with his primary BSSA contact, Katherine Hill, preoccupied with the Montreal operation to capture [[Syd Foxglove]], is struggling to interpret this evidence. He likely views the Grubs through his established RONIN framework, suspecting they are either a catastrophic failure of BSSA's [[IO (Emerging 1997)]] project or, more alarmingly, evidence of an entirely new, uncontrolled emergent entity linked to the [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]]-[[ErgoMatic Manufacturing]] network.
* **Fear of Exposure and Bureaucratic Self-Preservation:** The entire situation in Sequoia Falls—BNJC's persistent meddling, the clear entanglement of critical infrastructure, the volatile Foxglove legacy, BSSA's messy operations (like the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)|COTU]] F7 incident), and now Chrysalis's arrival with alien biologicals—represents an enormous liability for Caraway. He fears this escalating chaos threatens to expose years of questionable methods, potentially illegal activities (including his own CIA-backed consolidation efforts), and the fundamental instability of BSSA's approach under Hill. His primary motivation is shifting rapidly towards containment and bureaucratic self-preservation, terrified that the Sequoia Falls powder keg will ignite and destroy his standing within the intelligence community.
* **"The Departing Flight" Protocol:** This fear is pushing Caraway towards drastic measures. PCBOT maintains a contingency known as "[[PCBOT Headquarters|The Departing Flight]]"—a protocol for a thermobaric airstrike aimed at the complete obliteration of [[Sequoia Falls, WA]]. The pre-fabricated cover story blames the destruction on the spontaneous detonation of long-forgotten LTX freight cars filled with mining explosives from the [[CALLISTO (1949)]] era, supposedly located in old rail tunnels near COTU. This protocol represents Caraway's ultimate solution to erase evidence and contain the escalating crisis.

## Significance and Future Role
PCBOT, under Caraway, has transformed from an oversight body into a significant antagonistic force, driven by a desire for centralized control and now, increasingly, by fear. Their headquarters is set to become a crucial investigation site for the players as they seek to rescue Agent Chrysalis. This "PCBOT Excursion" will likely lead to a direct confrontation with Caraway and the activation of "The Departing Flight" protocol, creating a high-stakes climax where the fate of Sequoia Falls hangs in the balance. The organization also holds vital information, such as details on the [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC's]] "Ant Trap" protocol, within its archives. PCBOT's containment protocol, should its own internal security be breached, is ironically named "The Scenic Route," activated by a disabled hotel key card.

PCBOT embodies the dangers of centralized power, bureaucratic paranoia, and the attempt to impose conventional intelligence structures onto phenomena that defy such frameworks.
