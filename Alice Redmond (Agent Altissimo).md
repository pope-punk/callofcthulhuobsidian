## Overview
Alice Redmond, known within the [[Mirror-Breaker Organizations|Mirror-Breaker network]] as Agent Altissimo, is a former FBI agent renowned for her intelligence and resourcefulness. Recruited into the [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)|Blue Note Jazz Collective]] around 1990, she was a promising and effective operative, making significant progress on investigating the [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]] event and its aftermath throughout the mid-1990s until her investigation was shuttered by the [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)]]. No longer able to continue her collaboation with [[Éric Blanc]] on EUROPA, she turned her investigation towards Sequoia Falls and LTX, forming a friendship with [[Syd Foxglove]]. This connection helped the Collective circumvent the increasing bureaucratic roadblocks established by PCBOT after 1994 and access the company's records.

Following Syd's disappearance in early 1997, Alice (in collaboration with Director Nine-String) began investigating the activities of Sequoia Falls' PCBOT liason, [[Dylan Hill (Agent Destination)]], based on a tip from the deposed Jacqui Lopez that his wife, Katherine, was known to have recently deployed to Sequoia Falls. Tailing Hill throughout the late spring and early summer of '97 led to her discovery and documentation of an apparent collaborative network existing between Saint Anselm's, Seventh Tabernacle, and the Hammerskulls. However, before she could prepare a formal report, she saw Dylan transferring the kidnapped [[Syd Foxglove]] from [[Saint Anselm's Center for Hope]] back towards [[Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life]]. After intercepting Hill and freeing Syd, she interrogated Dylan. During his failed escape attempt and struggle for his life, he managed to stab her with a full syring of Fluoroxepram, resulting in a massive overdose of the experimental chemical [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]], which left her cognitively impaired and dangerously unpredictable. As of late October 1997 Alice operates as a rogue agent, driven by a fractured perception of reality, hunting what she believes to be components of a new and insidious RONIN at LTX, which are, in fact, [[Rhea]]'s [[Signal Dummies]].

## Early Life
Alice was born in 1966 to Clintock and Ondaline Redmond in Los Angeles, California. The child of an unlikely marriage between a disaffected Marxist-Leninist activist (Ondaline) and a disillusioned FBI agent (Clintock), Alice became the new locus of idealism for both formerly ideologically-motivated parents. 

Consequently, she grew up under significant pressure to please her parents, both of whom now saw her, rather than communism or patriotism, as their future. With signficant financial resources available on both sides of the family tree, they saw to it that their daughter's talents were nurtured in every regard: she attended extensive music lessons, received daily tutoring in advanced mathematics and Greek philosophy, and competed in equestrianism, swimming, and shooting. 

This left her with little time for normal social development, a tension which would persist through her enrollment at Berkeley in 1983 - having just turned age 17, she was at least a full year younger than her freshman classmates. 

In turn, she relied on connections from her parents' Ivy-league backgrounds for social connection, and which intersected precisely in criminology (her mother having gone into the legal field as a prosecutor). As a consequence of this arrangement, her friend group ultimately consisted of professors (a combination of impressed professors and her parents' professional contacts) and a circle of computer hackers, who repected her mathematical accumen (and were happy to be seen talking to a girl). This positioned her well to become a prime candidate for FBI recruitment when she graduated early in 1986 - socially unsavvy in most ways but on the cutting edge of mathematics and technology. 

With J. Edgar Hoover dead and buried, and both parents aglow in the neoliberal optimism of the 1980s, they gave their blessing for Alice to bring the Redmond name back to the FBI's roster. 
 
## FBI Career
Alice completed her training at Quantico on the same week that she turned 21. Having acquired a reputation as a consummate wunderkind, she was quickly attached as an investigator to an active case being investigated out of the FBI's San Francisco field office. Thus Alice's career began with distinction, as this assignment would culminate in her capture of the notorious [[Edmund White|Balloon Mask Killer]], whose reign of terror spanned the entire West Coast and ended at [[Edmund White Estate|Edmund White's vacation property]] in the woods southwest of Route 90. This success, and particularly Alice's usage of statistical methodologies, brought her to the attention of the Mirror-Breaker network, leading to her recruitment into the BNJC (then located in Sacramento) around 1990. Her unique background in mathematics and music made her a natural choice. At around this time, she began renting a house in Sequoia Falls, which she would later buy in 1993. 

## BNJC Sacramento
Redmond began her 14-month Mirror-Breaker training course in July of 1990 and concluded in September of 1991. In addition to rigorous training in the metaphorical cipher of the Collective, she also completed a curriculum based on that of the IHRU. It was around this time that she became close to [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nigel Christmas]] - she struggled to form friendships with the other agents her age (particularly Agent Legato, a few years older than her), and instead lapsed into her established pattern of bonding with authority figures over her peers. For his part, Christmas came to see her as a daughter in a way that somewhat mirrored her parents' perspective - her wild talent came to signify, in some way, the future of the organization. 

Her Sacramento casework for the BNJC largely revolved around ensuring that upper West Coast tech firms were continuing to observe randomization protocols in their growing IT systems. Until the end of 1992, Alice would regularly conduct site visits to the offices of IBM, Pacific Telesis, and others to meet with top engineers, gather data, and scan for evidence of potential RONIN emergences. During this period, she identified and an helped pre-empt a possible [[non-Jovian RONIN]] (no verified presence of the Foxglove Algorithm), termed Cadence 11, that was forming in an unregulated telephone subsystem within Pacific Telesis - a subsystem which had been used as a dial-up network by cutting edge technology firms to escape Mirror-Breaker oversight. 

This victory was seen as a win for the traditionalist approach to Mirror-Breaking in the context of the broader ideological rivalry playing out within the Mirror-Breaker network, but came at the cost of antagonizing the Collective's rivals. Compliance with randomization protocols was difficult and expensive for rapidly expanding tech companies, and the notion of RONIN without clear connection to the Foxglove algorithm had long been tendentious even among those Mirror-Breakers most aligned with the Protocol. Well-connected Silicon Valley moguls pressed on their contacts in the CIA and State Department to remove some randomization regulations, a request which ultimatley be granted in 1994. 

## EUROPA investigation
Following the [[EUROPA (1986-1992)|EUROPA]] event and the subsequent factional struggle, BNJC  relocated from Sacramento to Sequoia Falls to continue the investigation initiated SDEC had initiated into the Echo. Audio analysis of recorded episodes of Axon and Dendrite revealed revealed the existence of an apparent feedback pattern between voice pitch and the pitch of a background tone which played during the call-in section. Beyond that, the background tone caused distinct fear and disorientation in BNJC researchers, strongly suggesting that it was a Basilisk, potentially rooted in the Algorithm - although weak, this was still the most directly psychologically affecting Basilisk they'd encountered since Ganymede. While other analysts (primary among them Agent Chrysalis) turned their attention back towards the Foxgloves and LTX, Alice turned towards field work. Using Operation POUTINE records, she began investigating the numbers stations, beginning with the one in Ellensburg. Finding it stripped of all useful evidence, she turned her attention to other known POUTINE locations, soon meeting with [[Éric Blanc]] via her FBI connections. 

The two bonded very quickly and spent much of early 1993 traveling to a variety of locales in the POUTINE network, interviewing locals and attempting to determine if any transmissions of Axon and Dendrite had been broadcast from these stations. Of the six numbers stations visited, they found that the station in Juneau, Alaska had transmitted the show starting in 1991 and continued until its sudden end in 1992. 

During this time, the two became romantically entangled. Alice became very attached to the older Canadian, who, for his part, did his best to manage her expectations. Nonetheless, the two unofficially dated for much of late 1993 and 1994.  

Unable to find actionable evidence in Alaska, the pair returned to Sequoia Falls to re-visit the show's financial statements and re-examine the SCAI financials for evidence of purging: clearly expensive signal relays carrying Axon and Dendrite to Juneau had been either built or activated. However, shortly after interviewing [[Chrissy Perkins]] in late 1994, the EUROPA investigation was suddenly and officially transferred to New Orleans Photography Group as part of the sweeping changes implemented in the wake of Caraway's coup. At Nine-String's direction, Alice discontinued her collaboration with [[Éric Blanc]], but encouraged him to unofficially continue the investigation, which he did. However, they "broke up" as a result of their work together ending, and contact would remain limited as a result of hard feelings, particularly on Alice's part.

## Sequoia Falls Stalemate
After Caraway's coup, all corporate records requests had to be routed through a PCBOT liason. This enormously complicated BNJC's operational tasks. With EUROPA investigation and records officially transferred to NOPG, and LTX data filtered through Dylan Hill, the BNJC was organizationally hamstrung from late 94 through 95. Even though the investigation of Axon and Dendrite had ultimately led back to Ellensburg,  

Taking an investigative approach that was by that point characteristic, Alice leveraged agent Chrysalis' contacts with the Foxglove family (carried over from his time at SDEC) to connect with Syd, mostly unbeknownst to the rest of BNJC (and especially unknown to Hill). Although she had no concrete reason to believe that Dylan was censoring anything from the LTX shipping records, the sudden shuttering of the investigation, the apparently expensive nature of Axon and Dendrite that extended far beyond its reported funding, and the context of PCBOT's position in the theoretical rivalry between the Jazz Collective and the Sculpture Society created an impetus for Alice and Nine-String to attempt to circumvent the bureaucracy. 

Throughout mid-late 95 and 96, Syd helped Alice access LTX data without any potential filtering from Hill, which they surreptitiously transferred to their analyst team. In addition to hoping to prove that PCBOT was intentionally obstructing their investigation, they'd also hoped to find anything useful which could shine further light onto EUROPA (which they knew had been rooted in the Foxglove Algorithm) or at least be a potential monitoring site for any signs of its re-emergence. Neither line of investigation proved fruitful, but they continued to cross-check Hill's LTX data with the data they received from Syd as a matter of course. 

Outside of work, Alice spent time with Syd and his girlfriend Amanda Green during these years. Alice and Syd formed a quick friendship, having in common not just a love of music but also the strenuous social and parental expectations that came with their respective exceptional sets of talents. On another level, Alice was in a new and exciting position of having a friend that was actually young and cool, which Nine-String very much encouraged, harboring a distrust of her relationships with older men such as Blanc after he'd broken Alice's heart. 

## Syd's Disappearance and Alice's Investigation
The events of January, 1997 had a profound impact on Alice. Her only real friend had disappeared, the sole witness to a high-profile murder. Although she'd never met [[Oliver Foxglove]], Alice immediately knew that Syd could not have been responsible for his death, and she began inquiries into the Kittitas County case files, which she was initially denied, then later granted what she correctly deduced to be doctored form. The official story was, by the end of January, a "fatal car crash" on the police report, with no mention of Syd, and any formal search for him had ended. 

Powerless to intervene directly, she began tailing Dylan Hill throughout the first half of 1997, again with the blessing of Nigel - a blessing secured by a tip he'd received from Jacqui Lopez that Katherine Hill was reportedly active in Sequoia Falls. Over the following months, she documented his movements regularly, observing him traveling from BNJC to the White Stag, the Center for Hope, and the Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life, The Conifers, and on I-90. However, without evidence of clear malfeasance, her photographs weren't actionable. During this time, she obtained photographs of the interior of the Center for Hope, in which she identified the presence of several marble statues, strongly suggesting the involvement of Boise. Suspecting surveillance (though incorrectly), Alice covered up the statues in her home (gifted to her by BSSA) with blankets.   
## The Turning Point: July 1997
In early July, Dylan informed the Collective that he'd be returning to Washington, DC. His final visit to the collective occurred on July 13th. On July 15th, Alice was following him once again, and this particular instance would prove decisive, as this was the day that Dylan had been tasked with returning Syd from the Center for Hope back to the Church of Life. When Alice saw this occurring, she immediately sprang into action, ambushing Agent Destination on north I-90.  

Recognizing that Syd was not safe, she dropped him off at the bus stop near the I-90 overpass with instructions to head to Montreal and meet with Eric Blanc until she could contact him. She then took Dylan to the abandoned [[Edmund White Estate]] where she interrogated him. Placing him in his own PCBOT handcuffs on a chair in the basement, she attempted to obtain information Syd's captivity, but Dylan refused to give answers beyond "his detention is related to a PCBOT investigation of LTX" unless he were allowed to speak to Director Hotel first. Disbelieving this lie, Alice refused to allow Dylan to speak to Caraway, and slowly escalated towards torture. Dylan attempted to substantiate his story with reference to the anomalous LTX records he was carrying, but failed to satisfy his captor. He also failed to provide an explanation for the needles full of F7 he had in his suitcase. As Alice prepared to begin the next round of interrogation, Dylan slipped a hand out of a handcuff and a struggle ensued, during which he injected her a fluoroxepram syringe. However, due to the fact that she was taking prescription adderall, and with some help from her Mirror-Breaking training, she maintained enough cognitive acumen to avoid immediate incapacitation and managed to kill Hill in the struggle, bludgeoning him to death with her saxophone.  
## The F7 Fugitive: A War Against Shadows
As the systematizing capacity of her brain began to buckle beneath the chemical pressure of her massive exposure, Alice began forming associative lateral connections, putting together an incomplete picture of a RONIN at LTX which had been somehow concealed by Hill. Resolving to immediately head towards the nearest address on the list, she traveled by car towards I-90, and entered the Center of the Universe, following the Hexacles which were now visible to her. The concept of an "address" as a point on a spatial grid was becoming increasingly tenuous for her, and she interpreted the emergence of these strange shapes as some kind of "guide", indicating lines of flight between locations which operated beyond the logic of roads and interesections. 

It was here that she met a number of the denizens of the Center of the Universe but obtained relatively limited information about the Hexacles, and what she learned was filtered through the metaphorical lens of F7, essentially affirming her metaphysical interpretation of the symbols. She was told that they led into the tunnel and connected to a guardhouse where the Rubber Man lived. This was Thermos Milkshaft, the first Signal Dummy Alice would encounter and destroy. It resisted small arms fire from her service weapon and she ultimately ended up handcuffing it to the desk in the chamber before she returned to her vehicle as her adderall was wearing off. She made a trip to her home, gathered the last of her adderall and satellite phone, but was unable to access her safe - her F7-addled mind was unable parse the concept of a code, and intuitive pathways to opening the safe did not yield anything workable. She was also unable to call the BNJC for similar reasons. She returned to COTU, where a less-impaired resident helped her utilize the satellite phone to contact BNJC, but she was unable to communicate information beyond her location and her need to continue her anti-RONIN operations outside the purview of official oversight - she coordinated a first meeting with Agent Chrysalis at I-90 to obtain food and ammunition. She also used the satellite phone to contact Eric Blanc and confirm Syd had arrived in Montreal, and obtained a burner phone, a number which she wrote down with assistance.  

From July to September 1997, Alice, operating from the [[Edmund White Estate]], used Dylan's manifests to hunt and destroy numerous [[Signal Dummies]], believing them to be components of a new, dangerous RONIN. Her compromised state led her to tragically kill LTX employees [[Sarah Prescott]] (August 10th) and [[Thomas Reeves]] (September 8th), mistaking them for Dummies. She marked these sites with spray-painted [[The Hexacle|Hexacles]], perhaps a confused echo of their significance or a desperate attempt to impose order on a world whose patterns she could no longer decode. Throughout this period, she maintained covert contact with [[Agent Chrysalis]], who facilitated secure communications with [[Syd Foxglove]] as he began research into the [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] antidote.

A [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Reaper]] attack at the White estate on September 8th forced her to relocate her base of operations to the [[Sewers and Maintenance Tunnels|tunnels]] beneath the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)|Center of the Universe (COTU)]] encampment. [[Rhea]] then appeared to target her further via [[Calvin Pierce]]'s distribution of pure F7 at COTU in late September, an event which led to mass casualties among the homeless population. Alice, discovering the devastation and Calvin Pierce's subsequent murder by a [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Writer]], retreated deeper into the tunnels. Her killing of [[Sean Fielding]] around this time was another tragic consequence of her F7-induced misidentification.

## Current Status and Mental State (Late October 1997)
As of late October 1997, Alice Redmond is a rogue agent, hiding in the [[Sewers and Maintenance Tunnels|sewers and maintenance tunnels]] near [[COTU (Center of the Universe)]]. She is severely impaired by the F7 overdose, her perception of reality distorted, and her mission to destroy the "LTX RONIN" all-consuming. She is a figure of immense tragedy—a brilliant agent brought low, her formidable skills now guided by a fractured mind. She killed [[Pastor Mike]] at the [[Redwood Shopping Mall]] on October 7th during an encounter with the investigators, further demonstrating her dangerous instability. She dropped her satchel during this escape, which contained vital clues for the investigators, including her journal and keys to various important locations. She is in desperate need of the [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] antidote that Syd Foxglove is trying to develop.

## Future Role: Redemption and Sacrifice
The campaign's trajectory positions Alice for a potential, albeit costly, redemption. Once the investigators secure [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] and administer it, her cognitive functions and memories are expected to be restored. This lucidity will be crucial, as she holds key information: the vivid recollection of [[Agent Chrysalis]]'s panicked flight to [[PCBOT Headquarters|PCBOT HQ]] after the COTU incident. Alice will understand Chrysalis's complex motivations (his SDEC trauma, fear for BNJC, love for her, and his desperate gamble to expose or neutralize [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)|PCBOT]]) and recognize the grave danger [[Henry Caraway (Agent Hotel)|Director Caraway]] poses.

Alice will accompany the investigators to Washington D.C. to rescue Chrysalis, whose knowledge as the last SDEC field agent is vital for accessing the sealed [[SDEC Facility]]. During the climax of the "PCBOT Excursion" at their headquarters, as Caraway initiates the "[[PCBOT Headquarters|Departing Flight]]" airstrike protocol against Sequoia Falls, the investigators will deduce that enhanced pattern recognition (via [[G5 (Gallium-stabilized Amphetamine Complex)|G5]] or G6) is needed to decrypt the strike frequencies. Alice, understanding their plan and her unique position, is expected to volunteer for this perilous chemical decryption.

Her fate then diverges based on the chemical used:

* **Pathway 1 (G5):** If she uses PCBOT's [[G5 (Gallium-stabilized Amphetamine Complex)|G5]], she successfully decrypts the codes, allowing [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nine-String]] to jam the airstrike with the [[BNJC Radiometry Array]]. She escapes with the team but succumbs to fatal gallium poisoning during the journey back to the West Coast, allowing for final moments of clarity and interaction.
* **Pathway 2 (G6):** If she uses the players' [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]], she again enables the jamming of the strike but then enters the passive, automaton-like state associated with [[Rhea]]'s "Induced Susceptibility." She becomes unreachable and cannot be convinced to leave the facility.
* **Pathway 3 (G6 + F7):** If she takes G6, enables jamming, and enters the automaton state, a subsequent dose of F7 might temporarily revert her to baseline lucidity, allowing her to escape with the team. However, this volatile G6/F7 interaction would prove fatal, leading to her death with similar timing to the G5 pathway, offering a poignant end to her arc.

Alice Redmond's journey is one from dedicated agent to impaired hunter, and potentially, to a figure of tragic sacrifice, her actions pivotal in confronting the intertwined threats of human conspiracy and cosmic horror.
