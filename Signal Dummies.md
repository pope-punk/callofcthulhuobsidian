
## Description

**Signal Dummies** are humanoid automatons developed at [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing]]'s EAS division, serving as the sensorimotor interface or nodes in [[Rhea]]'s emerging technological consciousness substrate. They appear as mannequins with limited mobility, primarily synthetic but with biological components. They require regular hydration to maintain functionality. Due to [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)]]'s suppression of pattern recognition, [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)]] struggles to reliably distinguish them from humans. They often use absurd cover identities (e.g., "Thermos Milkshaft," "Alphonse Spicecube," "Quentin Cinnabon") for activities like shipping.

## Function & Purpose

* **Network Nodes**: Act as sensorimotor nodes, receiving instructions and executing tasks within Rhea's LTX-ErgoMatic network.
* **Information Relay**: Their primary activity involves shipping dirt packages, which create activation patterns maintaining Rhea's neural network. Dirt shipments are linked to activating this network.
* **Instruction Reception**: They consume [[Spectator Grubs]] to receive new DNA-encoded instructions, which are translated into machine code via an internal DNA-Machine Code Conversion System. This system prioritizes input from biological [[Spectator Grubs|Grubs]] delivered by [[Watchers]].
* **Physical Replacement**: Represent Rhea's planned physical replacement for humanity as part of its lifecycle's "Replacement Phase". They are predictable automatons suited for the Algorithm-driven econosphere.

## Deployment & Vulnerabilities

* **Deployment**: Progressively installed from remote locations (abandoned offices, storage units) post-merger (Feb-July 1997). Often found near water sources needed for hydration. Locations often marked by [[The Hexacle]].
* **Deactivation/Destruction**: Can be temporarily disabled with cattle prods (disrupts hydraulic control) or permanently destroyed through complete dehydration (takes days). Alice Redmond employed both methods.
