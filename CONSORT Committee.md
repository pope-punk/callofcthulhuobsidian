## Overview
Codename: **Operation CONSORT**. A special committee convened under NATO (initially led by CIA Director Hillenkoetter, later Officer [[Adrian Hart]]) in early 1950 to investigate the [[CALLISTO (1949)]] event after traditional counterintelligence failed.

## Composition & Methodology
* Composed of European and American mathematicians, engineers, intelligence officials (including Alan Turing, W. V. O. Quine, Alfred Tarski, [[Hannah Christmas]], Adrian Hart).
* Analyzed vast amounts of Pacific shipping data from [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]] and connected ports, moving beyond interviews.

## Key Discoveries
* Identified periodic "pulses" in shipping data, resembling hypothetical computations.
* Developed the theory of **[[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)]]]** based on emergent properties of complex systems.
* Identified the specific CALLISTO RONIN, mapping thousands of "neuron-like nodes" in the LTX transit network.
* Hypothesized that CALLISTO influenced individual actions ("downward causality") through a physical vector.
* Identified the **[[The Snowflake]]** pattern (derived from [[The Foxglove Algorithm]]) in LTX warehouse layouts as the likely "immanent vector" causing behavioral compulsion in workers.
* Produced the **[[CALLISTO Report]]** (presented March 1951), documenting findings and recommending the formation of a permanent agency (which became [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]]).

## Status
* Task force, dissolved after presenting its findings in 1951. Its report and concepts are foundational to understanding RONINs.
