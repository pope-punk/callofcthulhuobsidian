## Overview

**Polybius** was an arcade game built in the early 1980s by Andrew Chamberlain — later [[Anselm Chambers (Deacon)|Deacon Anselm Chambers]] — using principles he'd lifted from [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] documentation he found in LTX's corporate archives. The game functioned as a low-grade applied basilisk: it produced specific cognitive and perceptual effects in players (trance states, behavioral suggestibility, time loss) that Chamberlain used to seed and maintain a cult audience in the Seattle area.

Polybius is the urban legend the campaign's fictional setting is using as its historical "first weaponized Algorithm-derivative pattern outside the Foxglove line." Important not because it was particularly dangerous — it wasn't, on the CALLISTO/GANYMEDE/EUROPA scale — but because it proves that the Algorithm's basilisk-generating capacity is *accessible to anyone who finds the documentation* and willing to build with it.

## Effect

Specific psychological and perceptual effects on prolonged players. Time loss. Compulsion to return to the cabinet. Suggestibility around peer reinforcement (the cult). Not violent, not amnestic, not Echo-style hallucinatory — more like sustained mild trance with deepening engagement.

## What it produced

* A Seattle cult of devoted players in 1983-84 that BSSA raided in 1984.
* The first BSSA recruitment of Chamberlain as an asset (his "death" during the raid was staged with Katherine and Caraway's help; see [[Anselm Chambers (Deacon)]]).
* The applied-basilisk experience Chamberlain later brought to [[Operation Colossus]] (caller-feedback modulation of The Echo) and to [[Operation Parthenon|Parthenon]] (the Fauxflake geometry).
* Chamberlain's lieutenants [[Jacob Wise]] and [[Erin Cross]] — both originally Polybius cultists, recruited into BSSA's Ellensburg operation in 1986.

## Status

The cult was broken in 1984. Surviving cabinets are dispersed and presumed mostly destroyed; whatever copies of the source code Chamberlain wrote have either been absorbed into BSSA's archives or destroyed. Polybius itself is not an active threat in 1997. Its legacy is Chambers.
