## 1. Unresolved canonical questions

These are concrete points where the canon (post-revision) is ambiguous or where I made a judgement call you may want to revisit.

### Montreal / Blanc / Museum

* **What is Chrysalis's role at PCBOT, exactly?** You explicitly said you hadn't decided. I've reframed him as narratively useful but not load-bearing for SDEC access — he can be alive, dead, broken, or a witness. The Rhea-vs-Caraway tension still works if he's just a captured agent Caraway misread. You may want to commit to a specific outcome before the PCBOT excursion is authored.
* **How does Blanc die in the McCord operation?** I framed his most-likely death as "during the shootout afterwards," which matches your direction. The variants worth considering: shot during the museum firefight; executed off-screen by BSSA once players are confirmed to be in transit; rescued and then dying of injuries en route home with Syd at his side (high pathos, also closes the Alice/Blanc loop).
* **Why is Alice in particular the only one who can execute the PCBOT bypass methodology?** I leaned on (a) she's the only person who knows the passphrase and (b) she's the only surviving practitioner of the bespoke 1996 procedure. Worth deciding whether you want a *concrete* operational element here — e.g., the bypass involves a live timing window keyed to her own biometric input, or a riff on her saxophone vibrato, or some other in-fiction Mirror-Breaker improvisation that *only she* can pull off. The current canon supports any of these.
* **Does the encrypted drive contain anything else worth recovering besides the PCBOT/SDEC material?** I left it minimal, but Blanc was a working PI for years; there's an opportunity for one or two evocative side-clues (e.g., his ongoing dossier on Operation Colossus stragglers, photos from Juneau, an unsent letter to Maria Chen).
* **What is the actual standing-protocol date at the museum?** Pinning a calendar date matters because BSSA's window is finite. The campaign currently sits in late October 1997; the most dramatic option is "the next protocol date is days away when players reach Montreal."
* **Plaster's competence ceiling.** I described her as good enough to fool casual contacts but with a subtle tell (warming up too easily to unknown correspondents). If a player attempts something genuinely creative — sending a coded message in Mirror-Breaker style — does Plaster decode it, fake decoding it, or panic? Worth a one-line rule.

### Foxglove / Oliver / Safe

* **Andrea Foxglove's actual birthday.** Now load-bearing. Pick an actual date and write it into the Andrea Foxglove page (currently just "1962, deceased"). This will also drive the Ylynthii-base-6 worked example you'll want to ship as a GM handout.
* **The vacuum-sealed reagent's name and properties.** I called it a "gene-altering chemical agent" per the New To-Do List, but a real in-universe label would be useful. Suggestions: an ErgoMatic internal designator like "BAD-12" or an Oliver-coined name. This matters because Syd will reference it on the page, and Katherine should plausibly want it back.
* **How thoroughly was Oliver's office "cleaned" after his murder?** Currently the Master Timeline says his hard drive was confiscated. The Cipher Notes survive at the facility because they're in Oliver's *old* files in the research archive, not on his desk — but you should confirm whether the players are recovering them as physical paper (your call) or as a recovered digital archive (less satisfying).
* **Where does Syd's spontaneous Ylynthii doodling actually show up *for the players?*** The Algorithm-realization mechanism is now central to Oliver's defiance, but the *players* learn this via Oliver's emails, not by inspecting Syd's old scratch pads. You may want a confirming artifact at ErgoMatic — a scanned notebook page, a margin in a printed schematic.

### Alice cure pathway

* **Time pressure on Alice's cognition.** F7 is a one-shot massive overdose ~3 months back. Per the established characterization she is "severely impaired." Worth deciding whether her cognitive degradation is *progressive* (so the players are racing) or *stable* (so they have time to do everything in order). The decision affects pacing for the entire Act II→III bridge.
* **Will G6 actually work without Syd present to oversee administration?** Implied yes, but the in-fiction "Induced Susceptibility" risk means there's a moral hazard. Worth a sentence in the G6 file clarifying whether Syd accompanies the cure or whether the team brings synthesized G6 to Alice with Syd staying at the lab.

### PCBOT / SDEC

* **The remote-terminal extract: what is actually retrievable.** I implied Winston's Memoirs are extractable via the remote session, with the on-site visit becoming optional. You may want to decide *what specifically* is not remote-extractable (e.g., the original handwritten Hexagon Pages, physical samples, the SDEC randomizer hardware) so the optional San Diego trip retains real stakes for completionist groups.
* **Caraway's fate.** Currently a high-stakes confrontation but no canonical outcome. You may want to mark whether his death is a planned beat, a player option, or off the table entirely (e.g., he survives, ruined, to be a recurring antagonist if you ever run a sequel).
* **What happens to Katherine after the McCord Museum operation?** The current canon says "potentially final confrontation" but doesn't commit. The downstream PCBOT excursion still works whether she dies, escapes, or is captured. Worth nailing down because Caraway's behavior depends on whether he's lost his most important ally.

### Endgame

* **The Watcher exchange.** Canon says the Watcher offers Rhea's ultimatum: give it G6, or be destroyed. You may want a more textured version where the Watcher offers something specific in exchange (transcendence, peaceful coexistence, etc.) so the players have a real temptation rather than a binary horror choice.
* **What happens if players give G6 to the Watcher.** Currently framed as "doom humanity." The actual mechanism — does the Watcher administer it via the water table, via airborne dispersal, via the LTX network? — is worth a paragraph somewhere.

## 3. General organizational observations

### What's working

* **The lore/concept layer is mature.** Algorithm, Hexacle, Cognitive Dialectic, Pattern Recognition, Consciousness & Emergence, Rhea, Ylynth, Scions, Seer Configuration, Royal Jelly, Traumatic Activation — these read as a coherent metaphysical system, the bones of the cosmic horror. They overlap by design (multiple lenses on the same machinery). You probably don't want to consolidate these.
* **The character roster is dense and structurally connected.** Faction membership, family ties, codenames, factional alignment are all consistent and traceable. The cross-link graph is the project's strongest feature.
* **The Master Timeline and Story Trajectory are doing most of the load-bearing work** for plot consistency. They should be the canonical references when other pages disagree — and they now agree with each other after the Montreal revision.
* **The Established Canon (session log) is short, factual, and lightly written.** Leave it alone unless a session contradicts something canonical; treat it as the record of play.

### What needs attention before the human-rewrite phase

* **Voice drift in the largest files.** The biggest, most concept-heavy files (Cognitive Dialectic, Pattern Recognition, Consciousness & Emergence, Rhea, Seer Configuration, Scions, Ylynth Civilization, IO (Emerging 1997), Operation Parthenon, Project Ozymandias, the BSSA/BNJC/PCBOT overviews) consistently bloom into Gemini-style prose: subheading-heavy, "It is important to note that…", "this dual-purpose…", "…serving Rhea's broader agenda." The information is correct but the voice is generic. These will absorb the most of your authorial rewrite time. Suggestion: pick one (e.g., Cognitive Dialectic) and rewrite it as a stylistic anchor; then run the others through and align.
* **Index files are vestigial.** `Key Characters.md`, `Locations.md`, `Factions & Organizations.md`, `Core Concepts.md`, `Entities.md`, `Corporate Entities`, `Historical Orgs (IHRU, CONSORT)`, `Mirror-Breaker Organizations` — these are mostly lists of links. In Obsidian, the graph view and backlinks already do this for free. Consider whether they're earning their keep, or whether a single top-level "Index" or "MOC" (Map of Content) would serve. I left them in place because they're harmless and may be a reading-aid for you specifically.
* **The empty-placeholder pattern.** I filled in roughly a dozen stubs that were referenced from elsewhere. A handful of single-character files (e.g., `Adrian Hart.md`, `Chris Lavin.md`, `Miranda Cook.md`, `Robert Toomey.md`, `Edwin Burberry.md`) are minimal one-liners. These are fine as cross-reference targets and don't need expansion until you need them.
* **Two "to-do" registries collapsed into one** (`GM Workplan.md`). The old `To-Do List.md` was a Gemini-generated production plan that contained generic project-management text ("Quality Control Framework", "Resource Allocation") which was not adding value. The `New To-Do List.md` had your authentic plotting in a single dense paragraph; its substantive plot points are now distributed across the canon files (and explicitly preserved in the canon revision commit).

### Mild redundancies worth being aware of

* **`Hercules Cluster.md` vs `Ylynth Civilization.md`.** The Hercules Cluster page has a strong narrative passage about the Ylynth fall that I think is more in your voice; the Ylynth Civilization page is the more analytical synthesis but has more Gemini bloat. Consider folding the Hercules passage into Ylynth Civilization (or vice versa).
* **`Foxglove Algorithm Documentation (Memoirs, List, Diagrams).md`** is an index page for Algorithm-related documents but only lists three items. It might disappear into `Key Items & Artifacts.md` without loss.
* **`F7, G5, G6 (Chemical Agents).md`** is a wrapper that just points to the three individual chemical pages. Same observation — could be retired.
* **`The Foxglove Family.md` and `Scions.md`** discuss closely related material from different angles (family history vs cognitive configuration). They're complementary, not redundant, but a brief cross-link note at the top of each clarifying the split would help a future reader.
* **The Echo / Fauxflake / Black Coral / Snowflake / Hexacle / Hexagonal Patterns** are individually well-defined but the relationships are described slightly differently across the lore pages. Worth one careful editing pass to make sure each basilisk's relationship to the Algorithm is described in *exactly* the same conceptual terms across the relevant pages.

### A note on the CALLISTO Report and Alice/Katherine pages

You called these out as already in your voice. After review I agree — they read distinctively from the rest of the corpus (the CALLISTO Report particularly so, with its 1951-vintage memo prose). They should be the **style targets** for the rest of the rewrite pass. Anything that sounds smoother / more PR-deck than CALLISTO probably wants editing.
