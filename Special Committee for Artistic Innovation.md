## Overview

The **Special Committee for Artistic Innovation (SCAI)** was a Washington state government arts initiative, verifiably established by state amendment in 1987. Ostensibly created to fund experimental art projects, it primarily served as a covert financial front for [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)]]'s [[Operation Colossus]] between 1987 and 1992.

## History & Operations

* **Establishment (1987):** Created via state amendment, housed in the state capital (Olympia). Provided legitimate bureaucratic cover.
* **Funding:** Received a modest state allowance, used to fund a handful of legitimate experimental art projects alongside covert funding for the "Axon and Dendrite" radio show.
* **Personnel:**
    * **Director:** [[Chrissy Perkins]] (Legitimate arts supporter, unaware of covert activities).
    * **Finance/Budgets:** Phoebe Jones (Actually BSSA Agent "Plaster", ensured unusual invoices related to Colossus were hidden).
    * Sylvia Hope (Committee Member).
    * Chris Wallace (Committee Member).
* **BSSA Connection:** Agent [[Raymond Vaughn (Agent Rococo)|Rococo]] introduced [[Maria Chen]] and [[David Brewster]] to the committee to secure funding for "Axon and Dendrite". Agent Plaster (Phoebe Jones) managed the finances to obscure BSSA's involvement and the funding for relay station equipment upgrades.
* **Dissolution/Merger (Post-1992):** Following the termination of [[Operation Colossus]] and [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]], the committee was quietly merged back into the Washington State Arts Commission. Agent Plaster purged incriminating financial records. Archived (incomplete) records may still exist.

## Significance

* Provided the financial mechanism and bureaucratic cover for [[Operation Colossus]].
* The committee's records (equipment purchase orders, personnel files, final payments) served as a crucial investigative path for [[Éric Blanc]] to link the A&D show to the reactivation of POUTINE relays and eventually locate [[Maria Chen]].
* [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)]] and [[Éric Blanc]] interviewed [[Chrissy Perkins]] in 1993/94 during their initial EUROPA investigation but found nothing suspicious at the time due to the purged records.
