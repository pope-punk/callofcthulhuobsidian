## Description
A clue discovered by the investigators: phone number **514-874-2313**.

## Discovery
* **Location:** Found written on a note in Alice's improvised base at the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)]] encampment.
* **Timing:** Discovered by the players around October 10th, 1997, amidst the evidence left after the [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)]] incident and the discovery of [[Sean Fielding]]'s and [[Calvin Pierce]]'s bodies. Left behind by [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)]] during her time based there.

## Significance
* **What It Is:** A burner line Alice arranged for emergency contact between herself and [[Éric Blanc]] regarding Syd's safety. It was *not* Blanc's primary residential line and does *not* resolve to his apartment address.
* **What It Is Not:** It is not, by itself, a pathway to Blanc's apartment. BNJC never had the address; the burner channel went through Blanc's voicemail.

## Compromise & Current Status
* **Intelligence Leak:** Almost immediately after the players reported the number during a debriefing at [[BNJC HQ|BNJC]], it was overheard by [[Steven Rober (Agent Shutter)|Agent Shutter]], a mole working for [[NOPG (New Orleans Photography Group)|NOPG]]/[[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]]. The leaked number did not by itself reveal Blanc's address; BSSA reached Blanc through Katherine Hill's parallel investigative trail (see [[Éric Blanc]]).
* **Current Usefulness:** Calling the number reaches a voicemail box now controlled by [[Phoebe Jones (Agent Plaster)|Agent Plaster]] under Blanc's identity, used to lure callers into email correspondence and steer them toward the [[McCord Museum]] ambush. Treating the line as a primary contact channel walks straight into Plaster's trap.
* **The Counter-Intelligence Path:** Players who instead reach Blanc's apartment via the [[Maria Chen]] / [[Brewster's Lockbox]] chain bypass the Plaster correspondence entirely and discover the ransacked apartment, the [[McCord Museum]] standing-meeting protocol, and Blanc's locked computer.
