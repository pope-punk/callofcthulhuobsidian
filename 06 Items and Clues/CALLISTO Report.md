## Overview

* Documents the synchronized port explosions of December 1949.
* Rules out conventional explanations (sabotage, act of war).
* Analyzes [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]] shipping data, identifying anomalous patterns and feedback loops.
* Develops the theory of **[[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONINs]]** as emergent intelligences in complex systems.
* Identifies the **[[The Snowflake]]** pattern in LTX warehouses as the likely **immanent vector** for CALLISTO, causing behavioral influence ("passive compulsion").
* Discusses concepts like reflection enabling self-awareness and potential "downward causality."
* Recommends formation of a permanent agency ([[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]]).

## The Full Report

**CODENAME: OPERATION CONSORT

Turing, Quine, Christmas, and Tarski et al.

Edited by Officer [[Adrian Hart]] 

As Presented to Special Convention of the National Security Council by Adrian Hart and Dr. Hannah Christmas  
March 1st, 1951  
  

Editor’s notes: 

This document represents the narrowest possible selection from the hundreds of pages of work produced by the [[CONSORT Committee|CONSORT]] team and their collaborators. Many omissions have been made in the interest of communicating and substantiating the results of the report in as succinct a manner as possible. Dr. Quine and Dr. Tarski were kind enough to review this abridged version and have affirmed their full confidence that it retains the sense and meaning of their original.  
  

**Executive Overview: 

Between December 6th and December 8th, 1949, a series of accidental explosions independently struck five ports in four countries in extremely quick succession. These incidents cost more than 1200 lives and caused an estimated 400 billion dollars worth of economic damage during the subsequent nineteen-day shipping freeze and forensic investigation. 

The synchronization of the explosions at key infrastructural sites initially suggested an act of war. This possibility was systematically ruled out, raising further, and perhaps more troubling, questions. The hypothesized timeline of events has been reproduced to illustrate the strangeness of what we now call the [[CALLISTO (1949)|Callisto Event]]. 

**Timeline

- 061535ZDEC49, Tacoma, WA: a silo overpacked with ammonium nitrate catches fire and detonates following a power surge through faulty wiring. 

- 061615ZDEC49, Vancouver, CA: an accumulation of particulate organics (flour) in a series of four grain silos reaches STP flashpoint, resulting in conditions ripe for the devastating dust explosion triggered by a smoking dockworker. 

- 070015ZDEC49, Oakland, CA: A fuel tanker explodes when static accumulation in a warehouse containing densely packed insulation materials discharges into a pump station during offloading.

- 071230ZDEC49, Perth, AU: improperly stored fireworks ignite as a result of a record-setting heat wave.

- 080115ZDEC49, Osaka, Japan: U.S. military hardware bound for Korea is incorrectly safety-checked during transfer from the U.S.S. Davenport to the U.S.S. Fitzgerald. 400 tons of explosives detonate, resulting in the loss of both ships. 
  

The preceding items are the result of meticulous forensic investigation and incident reconstruction by top scientists, engineers, adjusters and specialists. 

Beyond the severity of impact, the tragedy attracted immediate attention for other reasons. Attention should be called towards the apparent outward propagation from the initial accident location - beginning in Tacoma and moving outwards in the West, North, and Southern directions along and across the Pacific Ocean. 

Operation CONSORT took over in early Spring of 1950 when traditional counterintelligence methods proved unable to explain what increasingly appeared to be a statistically impossible combination of random events. Over the past year, we have reached the unavoidable conclusion that an active and present danger continues to present a concrete risk to national security. The nature of this threat has been the subject of cutting edge research by the top minds of the free world: its complexity eludes simple explanation. We ask for your patience and credulity.    

The broad contours of the investigation are reproduced below. 

**Initial Inquest

By December 10th, national intelligence services had determined that every exploded shipment had at one point or another passed through Tacoma, Washington during the month prior to the incident. Until that point, Port of Tacoma dock workers were represented by Local 850 of the International Longshoremen's Union, a labor organization under investigation by federal agents for possible links to the Soviet Union. The situation in Tacoma therefore took on an unavoidably high media profile, as six FBI agents were killed when the blast wave struck their temporary dockside field office, including Assistant Director Andrew Christmas. 

Investigation immediately proceeded on the assumption that the FBI had been the primary target of an act of retaliatory Bolshevik terrorism in defense of the ILU. 

Attempts to substantiate this theory quickly  of questioning did not get far - forensic and interview evidence was entirely consistent with accidental explosions. Suspicion quickly moved from labor representatives (many of whom were killed) towards the Foxglove family, owners and operators of the Land Transit Express line, train station, and several warehouses serving the port. 

Questioning the patriarch, Mr. Winston Foxglove, produced no tangible evidence that he was a fellow traveler of the Communist Party. A search of the Foxglove estate proved initially only that he is a peculiar genius of odd habit. 

A lack of cooperation between international authorities as well as national departments complicated attempts to get past the impasse in Tacoma. As the situation in Korea developed, the particular character of the incident in Osaka was suppressed in most domestic media outlets at the request of the Department of Defense. Military Police conducted their own investigation without the involvement of federal law enforcement.   

In spite of this obstacle, several recurring themes in the early interviews (in Tacoma and elsewhere) suggested a more than circumstantial connection between these incidents, and although Foxglove’s personal motivations were apparently unimpeachable, it became increasingly apparent that his company, Land Transit Express, was the common factor in each disaster - everything had passed through their warehouses at least once. Selections from these interviews are presented in appendix 1. However, absent a motive or even any apparent crime, the investigation stalled by the holidays.  

Although shipping had slowly resumed by early 1950, public pressure to break the investigative deadlock and safeguard Pacific supply lines. Public and political support for expanding the inquest was significantly agitated by the developing Communist threat in East Asia. In February, a bipartisan congressional committee formally transferred the investigation to a special team convened under the North Atlantic Treaty Organization, to be led by CIA director Roscoe Hillenkoetter.  The director’s involvement lasted only five months until the war broke out. With Pacific tensions having crescendoed into open war, the public was largely content with blaming the Communists and moving on. However, as readers with Naval intelligence connections now know, research secretly continued under the direction of Officer Hart with the approval of top NATO officials. 

**OPERATION CONSORT

Now codenamed operation CONSORT, the special committee consisted of European and American mathematicians, engineers, and intelligence officials. Rather than rely on interview data, CONSORT researchers pored over enormous amounts of Pacific shipping data in search of any detectable changes in the flow of materials through Land Transit Express and the connected ports.

To the enormous surprise of some, our research revealed “pulses” in the data - periodic increases and decreases of materials shipped through the supply network. These patterns began appearing roughly around the time of Japanese surrender and increased in amplitude, complexity and sharpness during the period from 1945 to the end of 1949.

The pattern was not limited to goods of any particular industry, nor did they apparently correspond in a simple way to any known sources of periodic trends in economic behavior (holiday shopping, inflation rates, the cyclical advance of fashions or technology, etc.) 

Nor could any external source for the apparent patterns be located - all shipments appeared real and legitimate, conducted by the expected intersection of commercial and consumer buyers and sellers. Striking, however, was the mathematical resemblance that these movements bore to the hypothetical computations described by Dr. Turing in his recent essay, Intelligent Machinery.    

**R.O.N.I.N.

The spontaneous appearance of order from the chaos of Pacific commodity markets is perhaps not quite as outlandish as it first sounds: it is well known that the laws of international commerce, mental operation, and behavioral science are all nevertheless physical laws, varied though they may be. It is not impossible that the juxtaposition of these laws within the context of our advanced networked society might produce effects altogether unexpected and perhaps horrifying to our unprepared understanding.  

This perspective informed the methodology of CONSORT from that point onward. Broadening the search for a nameable enemy, our team rapidly expanded to include neuroscientists, signals scientists, and electronics engineers. Employing these new and cutting edge mathematical techniques, CONSORT developed a new theory of the “Randomly Organized Neuro-Informational Network,” or [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONIN]]. 

To that end, the CONSORT team identified at least 6743 neuron-like ‘nodes’ in the networks of transit managed by Land Transit Express, though it seems almost certain that there must be several orders of magnitude more. Provisionally named “CALLISTO,” this collection of linked points in space and time seemed to contain every moment of the disaster. A delayed shipment in Odessa, a timely departure from Boise, a worker in Tacoma punching in a few moments late to miss a training on proper storage of explosives - vast chains of apparent coincidences piling upon each other to culminate in a singular moment of coordinated destruction.

  

This is an apparent impossibility from the limited perspective of the individual. Yet there is biological precedent for such a sophisticated mode of self-organization occurring across the social, neural and “economic” levels of a species. Consider, for example, colonial organisms, such as the ant or the bee. In many ways, it is appropriate to consider such animals as “collective entities” in a most literal sense, with each singular nest or hive being itself the real organism, while the individual insect itself plays only the role of a “cell” or an “organ.” Indeed, on the jungle floor, each ant simply follows the trail left by the insect ahead, and leaves behind it a chemical signal for the next in line. In this way, the body of the colonial organism is composed just as much from the individual ants as from the chemical signals between them. So we believe things to be with CALLISTO. 

  

**Problems Encountered

The CONSORT team hypothesizes that CALLISTO existed in and through the combined effects of human behavior distributed across many thousands or millions of individual actors. Although substantiated by empirical evidence, we still are in search of a model of causality which links CALLISTO as an effect to CALLISTO as a cause. Dealing with only the former, we can continue to work within the physicalist bounds of contemporary neuroscience - a sort of systems-functionalism. To explain and incorporate the causal role played by CALLISTO in the behavior of its constituent nodes, it is necessary to venture onto speculative terrain. 

The critical reader will note that it is one thing to claim that the life-process of our society spontaneously produces brainlike behavior within its various modes of economic organization - in fact, such a situation might even be somewhat unsurprising from a certain scientific standpoint. However, it is another matter entirely to attribute real actant capacity to what might otherwise be a strictly empirical “surface phenomenon.” 

Phrased otherwise, *why* did Chuck Arthur leave the valve on Pump 12 open fifteen minutes before Melvin Sedgwick would arrive? *Why* did Sedgwick wear his fleece coat into the North Warehouse, where it would gather the static that would jump and spark? How did CALLISTO “know” these things, and which did it “cause”? Is there even a difference?

The reader may at this point be struck by the sense that the present report is on the verge of proposing a metaphysical or psionic relationship between the mind of the individual and the “mind” of CALLISTO. It must be emphatically stressed that this is not the case. For the RONIN thesis to be coherent within known science, a strictly physical vector linking the hypothesized process CALLISTO to the real concrete actions of persons involved.

**The Snowflake 

The search for the “immanent vector” of CALLISTO led us back to Land Transit Express and to the earliest evidence gathered by the FBI. We turned a new, more attentive and critical eye to the interviews conducted with LTX workers in December of 1949. The hostile, interrogatory and moreover leading nature of the questioning notwithstanding, a repeated theme appeared in transcribed interviews: the sense of being automated.  
  
Although initially disregarded by CONSORT as an artifact of bad questioning practices by an agency which had already made up its mind about the answers, the recurring statement by LTX workers that they felt “passively compelled” at various times in the weeks, months and even years leading up to the explosion, beginning roughly when the new warehouse shelving layout was introduced shortly before America entered the Pacific Theater against Japan. 

Diagrams of the warehouse layouts recovered from Winston Foxglove are included in appendix 2. Note the visual effect of the image on the mind. 

The visual effect of the warehouse’s floor plan when viewed from the catwalk on the second floor, can apparently create a sense of “being forced” into certain types of actions. The actual layouts of the warehouses were apparently sequentially derived from the Foxglove Algorithm, developed by Winston to optimize Land Transit Express operations. So far as anyone is presently able to tell, the Foxglove algorithm is simply an iterative solution to microeconomic partial differential equations which arise in the typical operation of a logistics business. Based on these calculations, the warehouses’ layouts would dynamically evolve. Workers would constantly rearrange the shelving within the warehouses constantly in response to changes in shipment type and frequency, generating new variations on the original six-pointed fractal shape: the Snowflake. We believe this shape to be CALLISTO's "immanent vector." 

That the mechanism for solving an optimization question for warehouse architecture should produce particular psychological effects in those exposed to its geometry is suggestive of the way that the beeswax of honeycomb acts as a sort of “physical guide” for the behavior of bees. It is our suspicion, at this time, that it is not precisely the algorithm itself but rather the subject of its calculations (the economic flows of the Pacific coast) and the iterative character of its working which has imbued it with its apparently commanding influence upon viewers. That is to say, the simple fact that the algorithm took as both its input and output the timing and placement of shipping orders permitted a "working back" upon itself. An evolutionary feedback loop, then: the pattern and the people. The ants and the pheromones. A mirror for self-awareness.  
  
**Conclusions

We cannot yet say whether the emergence of self-organizing intelligence is a property of complex systems in general, of socioeconomic systems in particular, or of logistical systems specifically. We will comment, however, that CALLISTO’s apparent ability to self-coordinate across the biological, social, and spatial dimensions of society suggests that distinctions between these types of systems may not even be strictly valid. 

Whatever the case may be, the implications for national security are dramatic and far-reaching. Although CALLISTO appears to have essentially destroyed itself, treating it as a fluke or one-off event is in our opinion a disastrous error. 

It is unclear if CALLISTO is truly the first RONIN. The technological organization of human society may have only now reached the necessary level of complexity for CALLISTO’s emergence. However, the alternative possibility should not be discounted: that our species has until this point in history lacked the perspective or tools necessary to identify such “entities.” If a candidate RONIN event were to be identified in history, this would well-nigh prove the validity of our thesis. 

Of significant concern as well is the apparent susceptibility of the brain itself to direct influence by particular types of patterns in the visual field. Of course, on the one hand, this is not a discovery at all: written language has existed for more than 5,000 years. On the other hand, it is profoundly disquieting to learn that our species may be able to read far more languages than it is able to speak - is this the origin of the myth of the Basilisk, a myth in translation from the unknown and unspoken language of the occipital lobe? If the Snowflake can exert a compulsive effect, it remains a distinct possibility that other, potentially more severe, neurological effects could be unknowingly induced by similar fractal geometries.  
  
**Recommendations:  

The speculative nature of this document is well-known to its authors. If, however, its conclusions are convincing in the slightest, the CONSORT team has made several urgent recommendations for response to this threat. 

1. The expansion of CONSORT and its institutionalization as a permanent department embedded within the intelligence arm of the United States government. 

2. The immediate seizure of Land Transit Express by friendly elements within the business community and the clandestine decommissioning of the Foxglove algorithm. 

3. Immediate seizure by this agency of copies of all macro-scale shipping records from all ports operated by American companies, in as extensive and unredacted a condition as available.    

4. Commitment to extreme secrecy - the conclusions of this report may cause public alarm of the type which would make impossible the forms of social research required by the CONSORT team.

The Red menace has already provided ample reason for the expansion of intelligence-gathering for the purposes of safeguarding American interests. Should CONSORT be allowed to develop into a unit specifically aimed at combating informational hazards such as RONIN and their basilisks, two primary powers will be necessarily extended to it.

- Power of arrest, search and seizure, and all other powers presently available to the Central Intelligence Agency 

- Authority to conduct experimentation in the realms of the social, biological, and psychological with minimum civilian oversight
  
These suggestions may seem extreme. It is our sincere hope that the preceding report is sufficient to convince the reader otherwise.
