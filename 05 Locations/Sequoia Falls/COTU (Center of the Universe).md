## Overview
The "Center of the Universe," or COTU, was a sprawling, ad-hoc encampment of homeless individuals situated on the outskirts of [[Sequoia Falls, WA]]. For a brief period in the summer and early autumn of 1997, it represented a unique and desperate social experiment, a fragile community formed under the most unusual of circumstances. Its rapid formation, the bizarre collective behaviors exhibited by its residents, and its tragic, abrupt end are all deeply intertwined with the machinations of [[Rhea]], the influence of [[The Hexacle]] basilisk, and the catastrophic effects of the psychoactive chemical [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]]. For the investigators, COTU served as a crucial, if horrifying, entry point into the deeper mysteries of the campaign.

## Genesis: The Hexacle and a Cognitive Anomaly
The unlikely genesis of COTU can be traced to a perimeter of [[The Hexacle]] patterns meticulously painted by [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Rhea's Writer agents]] around the nearby [[Dead Horse Quarry]]—a site of immense significance to Rhea, likely concealing a primary [[Hive]] entrance. [[The Hexacle]], when viewed by individuals with baseline human cognition (i.e., those with the standard repression of [[The Foxglove Algorithm]]), induces a profound and often undetectable amnesia, effectively making the quarantined area "invisible" or experientially null.

However, a critical interaction occurred with individuals who had previously been exposed to low, ambient levels of [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]], a chemical known to suppress the [[Cognitive Dialectic (Metaphor vs. System)|System Pole]] of cognition and thus dampen pattern recognition. For these F7-affected individuals, the Hexacle's amnesia-inducing properties were negated. Instead of forgetting the area, they were inexplicably *drawn* to the perimeter, perceiving the Hexacle patterns not as a repellent but as a point of strange significance or fixation.

This phenomenon led to the initial gathering of a small number of homeless individuals, many of whom had likely been exposed to F7 through various means (perhaps as undocumented test subjects for [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA's]] early [[Project Ozymandias]] research or through contaminated local drug supplies). They began to congregate just outside the Hexacle-defined boundary, forming the nascent COTU encampment.

## Life in COTU: A Precarious Community
As more individuals, many already marginalized and suffering from mental health issues or substance abuse, were drawn to the site, COTU grew. It developed its own internal structure, with figures like [[Pastor Mike]] attempting to provide guidance and solace. The residents exhibited unusual behaviors, including a strange reverence for patterns and a detached, almost ritualistic quality to their daily lives. This was likely a result of the ongoing subtle cognitive effects of their prior F7 exposure interacting with the ambient influence of the nearby Hexacle field and, unknown to them, the deeper resonance of Rhea's proximity.

[[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)|Alice Redmond]], severely impaired by a massive F7 overdose, sought refuge in the [[Sewers and Maintenance Tunnels|sewers and maintenance tunnels]] beneath and around COTU after September 8th, using them as her primary hiding ground while maintaining a working base in a boxcar at the COTU surface (Section E of the encampment, see Investigative Layout below). The encampment sits in a ravine under the I-90 highway corridor, and the I-90 underpass adjoining it served as the resupply meet point where [[Agent Chrysalis]] dropped off grey rental Hondas, food, and ammunition for Alice between July and late September — without himself entering the COTU encampment until September 25, when the mass-death scene he witnessed there broke his support relationship with her.

## Investigative Layout (Oct 10, 1997)

By the time the players arrive on Oct 10, COTU is a ghost site — corpses and Hexacle graffiti, no living residents. The encampment runs the length of a ravine 10–15 feet wide, with tents stacked vertically up both walls in multi-story tarp-and-rope structures.

* **Section 0 (Approach).** Players enter the ravine disoriented after the [[The Hexacle|Hexacle]] perimeter; tents and trash on both walls, one starved body in a tent (prison tattoos, no wallet, traces of methamphetamine), rotting food, one tent with food hung from the ceiling like ornaments, a baggy of fine white powder mixed with meth.
* **Section A — LTX shipping container.** Partially buried, "LTX" stenciled. The peeled-back ends serve as makeshift doors; a tarp lean-to on top. **Calvin Pierce's body** lies half in / half out of the near end wearing a bomber jacket — multiple stab wounds, no starvation, a love letter, an unopened bag of methamphetamine. Inside: mattresses, crates, **manifests dated January 1950** (a CALLISTO-era artifact, atmospheric).
* **Section B — vertical tent complex** across the ravine. Rope bridge connects to the container roof. First floor: plastic event tables, sleeping bags, one starved body in a Saint Anselm's T-shirt. Second floor: six starved bodies, religious icons assembled from canned food, "GOD" scrawled on a tarp.
* **Section E — Alice's boxcar.** A separate sealed container Alice converted into her surface working base. Contains: a **dissected [[Spectator Grubs|Spectator Grub]]** (taken from the [[Signal Dummies|Thermos Milkshaft]] station and dissected for analysis) alongside a GenAssist-printed comparison; a scrap of paper with the **[[Montreal Phone Number]]** (514-874-2313); her handouts; her journal; Adderall bottles; F7-residue baggies. The boxcar smells. This is where players reconstruct Alice's investigative state — she had been actively reality-testing, and on the Grubs specifically she'd succeeded at the biology question (they're real) while her F7 frame still misclassified Rhea's biomorphic agents as hallucinations.
* **The underpass parking row.** Rank of identical **grey rental Hondas** with desiccated Signal Dummies in their trunks, plus three civilian outliers: [[Sarah Prescott]]'s Toyota, [[Thomas Reeves]]'s Nissan, and [[Sean Fielding]]'s Volkswagen Passat. Sean's trunk shows bloody fingernail marks where he clawed at the lid and two-pronged cattle-prod burns on the body.

## The F7 Poisoning Event and Aftermath (Late September - Early October 1997)
The fragile existence of COTU came to a horrific end in late September 1997. [[Calvin Pierce]], a Hammerskull associate with connections to [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]], distributed nearly pure [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]] throughout the encampment, possibly under the guise of a new potent drug. This act was likely orchestrated by [[Rhea]] (perhaps via BSSA manipulation or direct influence on Pierce) to neutralize a population whose F7-altered cognition made them resistant to the Hexacle's primary concealing effect, thereby potentially exposing the Quarry.

The mass overdose resulted in widespread death and catastrophic psychological breakdowns among the residents. Many succumbed to a paradoxical state of food-worship and starvation, a bizarre and extreme side effect of massive F7 toxicity. [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Writer agents]] subsequently moved in to repaint [[The Hexacle|Hexacles]] (likely to reinforce the Quarry's defenses now that the F7-resistant population was eliminated or dispersed) and to "clean up," which included the ritualistic murder of Calvin Pierce, his purpose served.

When the investigators arrived at COTU in early October, following leads from [[Emily Fielding]]'s search for her brother [[Sean Fielding]], they discovered a scene of desolation, lingering madness, and cryptic clues. Sean Fielding's body was found in the trunk of his car. They encountered the F7-impaired [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)|Alice Redmond]], battled [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Rhea's agents]], and gathered vital evidence pointing towards BSSA, the [[Montreal Phone Number]], and the deeper, weirder realities of Rhea's influence.

## Significance
COTU is a microcosm of the campaign's themes: the tragic consequences of hidden manipulations, the vulnerability of human cognition, the devastating impact of infohazards and psychoactive chemicals, and the desperate search for meaning and community in a world fractured by unseen forces. For the investigators, it served as a harrowing "point of no return," plunging them directly into the central mysteries and setting the stage for their subsequent confrontations with BSSA and their pursuit of Alice Redmond and Syd Foxglove. The clues gathered at COTU, particularly the [[Montreal Phone Number]] and materials from Alice's abandoned satchel, were critical in guiding their next steps.
