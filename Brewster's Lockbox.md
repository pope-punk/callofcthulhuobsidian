# Brewster's Lockbox

## Overview

A physical lockbox containing sensitive information left behind by the late [[David Brewster]] before his execution by [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]] in mid-1992. Accessing this lockbox is crucial for locating Brewster's co-host [[Maria Chen]] and uncovering deeper truths about [[Operation Colossus]]. Its retrieval is the reward for solving the [[Brewster's Lockbox Puzzle]].

## Physical Description

*(GM Note: Define appearance - e.g., a sturdy, unassuming metal document box, perhaps slightly worn, secured with a combination lock or a key lock requiring a specific key held by the keeper?)*
Example: A medium-sized, fire-resistant metal lockbox, dark grey, with a standard key lock. It looks practical rather than ornate.

## Origin & Keeper

* **Origin:** Prepared by [[David Brewster]] in his final days (mid-1992) as he realized [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]] was hunting him and likely compromised [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]].
* **Keeper:** Entrusted for safekeeping to a trusted friend or former colleague, likely located near Ellensburg (Placeholder: [[Professor Vance]]).
* **Brewster's Motivation:** Brewster needed to protect the location of [[Maria Chen]], who had survived the BSSA purge. He devised a security measure tied to the SDEC randomizer tool, believing only true SDEC allies could decipher the necessary passphrase, thus keeping the contents safe from BSSA.

## Acquisition Method

The lockbox cannot be opened directly; it must be obtained from its Keeper ([[Professor Vance]]). This requires:
1.  Successfully solving the [[Brewster's Lockbox Puzzle]].
2.  This involves finding both the **[[Brewster's Final Segment Sequence Log]]** and the **[[SDEC Randomizer Mapping Rules]]** at the [[Ellensburg Numbers Station]].
3.  Using the rules to reverse-engineer the specific **four-digit code** Brewster used for his final broadcast sequence.
4.  Locating the Keeper ([[Professor Vance]]).
5.  Providing the correct **four-digit code** to the Keeper as a passphrase. Recognizing this SDEC-linked code confirms the investigators are likely allies, fulfilling Brewster's security requirement.

## Contents

* **Primary Content:** The current (as of 1997) **contact information for [[Maria Chen]]** in [[Portland, OR]], likely including her assumed name, address, and possibly a phone number.
* **Secondary Content (Optional):**
    * A final letter or note from [[David Brewster]] explaining his fears or urging caution.
    * A small piece of physical evidence related to [[Operation Colossus]] or BSSA's activities that Brewster managed to save.
    * Perhaps the key to the lockbox itself, if it uses a key lock rather than combination.

## Narrative Role

* Serves as the tangible reward for completing the [[Brewster's Lockbox Puzzle]].
* Acts as a narrative gate, blocking direct access to [[Maria Chen]] until the investigators have pieced together the history related to [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)]], [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]], and Brewster's fate.
* Represents Brewster's final act of defiance and his attempt to ensure the truth about [[Operation Colossus]] could eventually be revealed by trusted parties.
* Provides the crucial lead needed to progress the investigation into BSSA's past actions and potentially gain insight into [[Éric Blanc]]'s prior involvement.

## Status (Late Oct 1997)

Held securely by [[Professor Vance]] (or designated Keeper). Locked. Awaiting retrieval by investigators who can provide the correct four-digit passphrase derived from the puzzle solution. It is listed as a **New** key item in the [[Key Items & Artifacts]] index.
