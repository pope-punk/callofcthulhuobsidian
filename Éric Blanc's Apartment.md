## Description

The personal residence of [[Éric Blanc]] in [[Montreal, QC]]. This is the **only** Blanc-associated location in Montreal — there is no separate safehouse. It served simultaneously as Blanc's home, his operational base, his storage site for sensitive materials from his EUROPA-era work with Alice, and (informally) the place [[Syd Foxglove]] lived after arriving in July 1997, before relocating to a small rental near McGill at Blanc's recommendation.

## Significance

Investigating the apartment is the primary mechanism by which the players come to understand:

* That Blanc has been compromised (captured, not killed in his home).
* That the [[McCord Museum]] is a standing fallback rendezvous point with Syd, and that the next date is imminent.
* That the BSSA "Blanc" email correspondence the players have been engaging with is a deception.
* That the apartment computer holds the **encrypted PCBOT tunnel schematics and a methodology for bypassing PCBOT defenses to gain remote terminal access to [[SDEC Facility|SDEC]]** — and that **only Alice Redmond can decrypt these files.** This is what makes curing Alice a required precondition for the PCBOT operation rather than an optional humanitarian goal.

## Current State (Late October 1997)

Players will find the apartment **nearly ransacked but not fully sanitized**. BSSA hit the place quickly to extract intelligence and capture Blanc, then left it in a state designed to spook any returning ally rather than to fully cover their tracks.

### Evidence Remaining

* **Wall Calendar:** Specific days each month circled in red, annotated with single-letter codes (e.g., "S" for Syd). Cross-references trivially with the museum receipts.
* **Museum Receipts / Membership:** A small folder of [[McCord Museum]] admission stubs and a Friends-of-the-Museum membership card. Dates on the receipts align with the circled calendar dates.
* **Personal Photographs:** Pictures of Blanc with Alice from 1993-94 (during the EUROPA fieldwork), confirming Blanc's identity and his relationship with Alice.
* **Notebooks (Partial):** Notes referencing the [[Operation POUTINE]] relay network, [[Maria Chen]]'s rough story, and the [[Special Committee for Artistic Innovation]] purge by Agent Plaster.
* **Signs of Struggle Not Murder:** Overturned chair near the front door, a coffee mug on its side, partial scuff marks. No blood. No corpse. Read together, the scene reads as a capture and forced exit.
* **The Computer:** Powered down, but intact. Boots to a login prompt; encrypted home volume requires a passphrase. Files include the **PCBOT tunnel schematics** and a **PCBOT-bypass methodology for remote SDEC access**, both encrypted. Only Alice knows the passphrase.

### Evidence Removed by BSSA

* All correspondence with Alice from 1994 onward (BSSA has copies).
* Any documents directly identifying Blanc's day-to-day routine with Syd.
* Phone records and address books, save for the calendar.

## Why Curing Alice Is Required

The PCBOT bypass methodology stored on Blanc's drive is not a passive piece of intelligence. It is operational — it requires:

1. **The passphrase to decrypt the files** (only Alice knows it).
2. **An understanding of the procedure encoded within**, which references the SDEC CICADA-class sonar mapping and a chain of compartmented Mirror-Breaker reasoning that, in 1996, only Alice and Blanc could navigate. After Blanc's capture and likely death, Alice is the sole surviving practitioner of the methodology.

Until G6 restores Alice's cognition, the schematics on the drive are useless and the players have no viable route into PCBOT's secure levels — let alone the means to reach SDEC. This makes the chain **Montreal → Rescue Syd → Synthesize G6 → Cure Alice → Decrypt Apartment Drive → PCBOT → SDEC** structurally necessary rather than incidental.

## Investigative Value

* **Counter-intelligence path:** The apartment is the *counter-intelligence* path. Finding it reveals the trap before the players walk into the museum blind.
* **Coordination point:** [[UMSE (Upper Michigan Succulent Enthusiasts)]] field agents may rendezvous here to support the McCord Museum operation.
* **Hardware:** The computer and the encrypted drive are recoverable; the players will need to physically transport them (or the drive) back to wherever Alice is being treated for decryption.

## How Players Reach the Apartment

The apartment address is **not** known to [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nine-String]] or anyone else at BNJC. The discoverable chain is:

* [[Brewster's Lockbox]] (via the [[Brewster's Lockbox Puzzle]]) → [[Maria Chen]] in Portland → Blanc's Montreal address.

(Alternative branch: the Amanda Green → Seattle friend → Chen chain established in [[Story Trajectory|Act II]] leads to the same place.)

The [[Montreal Phone Number]] found at COTU reaches a burner Blanc carried for emergency Alice contact only; it goes to voicemail under Plaster's control and is no longer useful as a primary entry vector once Chen is identified.
