## Overview
Dead Horse Quarry, formerly known as the Harrison Gold Prospect, is an abandoned mining site located on the outskirts of [[Sequoia Falls, WA]]. While appearing as a derelict and forgotten relic of the region's mining past, the Quarry holds profound significance in "The Cycles of Rhea." It is the site of [[Winston Foxglove|Winston Foxglove's]] pivotal 1922 childhood encounter with an agent of [[Rhea]], and, more crucially, it conceals an entrance to one of Rhea's primary subterranean [[Hive|Hives]] on the West Coast. The entire area is heavily protected by [[The Hexacle]] basilisk, rendering it effectively invisible and inaccessible to baseline human perception.

## A History Steeped in Gold and Secrets
The site's history begins as the Harrison Gold Prospect. After the gold veins were exhausted, it transitioned to other forms of mineral extraction before eventually being abandoned and falling into disrepair, becoming "Dead Horse Quarry."
* **Winston Foxglove's Encounter (1922):** The most significant historical event tied to the Quarry occurred in 1922. A young [[Winston Foxglove]], exploring the area, discovered a collapsed mineshaft or fissure that led him to an accessible part of [[Rhea]]'s [[Hive]]. It was here that an [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Editor agent]] administered [[Royal Jelly]] to him, initiating the genetic alterations that would define the [[The Foxglove Family|Foxglove Line]]. After this encounter, the entrance Winston used collapsed further or was deliberately sealed, preventing easy rediscovery.
* **SDEC Investigation (Pre-1992):** The [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|San Diego Entomology Club]], prior to its destruction, conducted investigations into anomalous phenomena in the Pacific Northwest. They detected unusual energy signatures and infohazardous patterns emanating from the Dead Horse Quarry area. Their research eventually led them to connect the Quarry's anomalies with [[Winston Foxglove|Winston Foxglove's]] memoirs, specifically his account of the 1922 encounter and the cryptic [[Hexagon Pages (Winston's Memoirs)|Hexagon Pages]]. This discovery proved fatal, as it brought SDEC too close to identifying the Quarry as a major Rhea nexus.

## Hexacle Defenses and Cognitive Invisibility
Dead Horse Quarry is not merely abandoned; it is actively concealed by [[Rhea]] using its most fundamental infohazardous defense: [[The Hexacle]].
* **Perimeter of Amnesia:** [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Writer agents]] have meticulously painted a perimeter of [[The Hexacle]] patterns within the Quarry and its access points. For individuals with normal human cognition (i.e., those with the standard repression of [[The Foxglove Algorithm]]), viewing these patterns induces a perfect, targeted amnesia. They forget having seen the patterns, forget the area itself, and are often unaware that any memory lapse has occurred. This makes the Quarry and the Hive entrance it conceals effectively invisible and experientially null to the vast majority of the population.
* **COTU Formation:** The strange interaction of these Hexacle patterns with individuals previously exposed to low levels of [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]] (which negates the Hexacle's amnesiac effect and instead creates a point of fixation) led to the formation of the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)|COTU]] encampment on the Quarry's periphery.
* **Reinforced Defenses:** Following the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)|COTU F7 poisoning event]] and the elimination of the F7-resistant population, [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Writer agents]] were observed reinforcing the Hexacle patterns around the Quarry, ensuring its continued concealment.

## Significance and Future Role: The Path to the Hive
Dead Horse Quarry's primary significance lies in what it conceals: a critical access point to [[Rhea]]'s [[Hive]].
* **Endgame Location:** The Quarry is expected to be a key location in the campaign's endgame. Investigators, likely after deciphering [[Winston Foxglove|Winston Foxglove's]] memoirs and the [[Hexagon Pages (Winston's Memoirs)|Hexagon Pages]] found at the [[SDEC Facility|SDEC facility]], will learn of its true importance.
* **Overcoming Defenses:** Accessing the Quarry and the Hive entrance will require the investigators to find a way to bypass or negate the effects of [[The Hexacle]]. This might involve using [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]] (with all its attendant risks), leveraging the unique cognitive traits of a [[Seer Configuration]] individual, or discovering some other method to disrupt the basilisk's effects.
* **Confrontation Site:** The Quarry and the Hive network beneath it are anticipated to be the setting for the final confrontation with [[Rhea]] or its most powerful agents, particularly if the investigators seek to disrupt its plans involving [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] or its attempt to fully integrate with the Earth's technosphere.

Dead Horse Quarry represents the threshold between the mundane world and the deep, alien workings of Rhea. It is a place where local history, industrial decline, and cosmic horror intersect, a forgotten corner of the world that holds the key to one of its greatest threats.
