## Overview
The Mirror-Breaker Protocol is a unique set of operational doctrines and underlying philosophical principles that were adopted by the various successor agencies, collectively known as the [[Mirror-Breaker Organizations]], which formed in the wake of the catastrophic [[GANYMEDE (1979)]] disaster and the dissolution of the [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|InfoHazard Response Unit (IHRU)]]. The protocol is specifically designed to combat the emergence and influence of [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks (RONINs)]] by disrupting the conditions conducive to their formation.
![[mbc.png]]
## Core Premise and Origins
The GANYMEDE event, which saw a RONIN emerge from the IHRU's own data analysis processes, provided a terrifying revelation: **systematic observation and the capacity for reflexive self-awareness within complex information systems can directly trigger RONIN emergence.** The Mirror-Breaker Protocol was therefore conceived as a radical departure from previous, more systematic approaches to infohazard research. Its fundamental aim is to **disrupt pattern formation, prevent stable systemic reflection, and thereby deny nascent RONINs the coherent feedback loops they require to achieve self-awareness.** The name itself evokes the core tactic: if a RONIN "sees itself" in the mirror of a system's operations, one must "shatter the mirror."

## Key Principles of the Protocol
The Mirror-Breaker Protocol is not a rigid set of rules but a guiding philosophy manifesting in several key operational principles:

1.  **Shatter Reflection:** This is the paramount principle. Agents and organizations must actively work to disrupt feedback loops within information systems and prevent any system—including their own organization—from achieving a stable, coherent self-observation.
2.  **Metaphorical Ciphers:** Instead of traditional, easily analyzable codes, operations and communications are to be conducted using division-specific, often eccentric and highly contextual, metaphorical languages. For example, [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)]] uses jazz terminology, [[UMSE (Upper Michigan Succulent Enthusiasts)|UMSE]] uses botanical terms, and [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] used insect behavior. This creates "interpretive discontinuities" that resist systematic decryption by emergent intelligences.
3.  **Embrace Compartmentalization:** Information is handled on a strict need-to-know basis. This prevents any single individual or group from assembling a complete, systematic picture of operations, which could inadvertently create a new reflective surface for a RONIN. As one tenet states, "Your ignorance may be a zero through which the circuit cannot be completed."
4.  **Process, Not Object:** The enemy (RONINs, and by extension, potentially [[Rhea]]) is understood not as a discrete, bounded entity but as an ongoing, emergent *process* that the organization itself is inherently a part of and can influence, positively or negatively.
5.  **Metaphor Over Code:** The protocol recognizes that simple symbolic equivalence (as found in traditional codes) is vulnerable to systematic analysis. Metaphors, with their ambiguity and reliance on context and interpretation, are believed to offer greater resistance to such analysis by pattern-seeking intelligences.
6.  **Embrace Unpredictability and Improvisation:** Agents are encouraged to act as "rogue thoughts" within the system, to improvise, and even to be a mystery to themselves at times. This operational fluidity aims to prevent them from falling into predictable patterns that a RONIN could learn to exploit or incorporate.

## Philosophical and Cognitive Links
The Mirror-Breaker Protocol has deep connections to the campaign's understanding of human cognition:
* It stands in direct contrast to more systematic and analytical thinking, which, while powerful, is seen as vulnerable. This is a point of contention with factions like [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]], which, despite its origins as a Mirror-Breaker organization, has increasingly adopted more systematic and interventionist methods.
* The protocol actively seeks to strengthen and leverage the **metaphorical and unpredictable pole** of the [[Cognitive Dialectic (Metaphor vs. System)|human cognitive dialectic]]. This is seen as a natural defense against infohazards and RONIN influence that primarily target or emerge from systematic, pattern-based processes.

## Effectiveness and Internal Conflicts
The true effectiveness of the Mirror-Breaker Protocol is a subject of ongoing debate and conflict within the remaining organizations:
* Figures like [[Katherine Hill]] of [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]] view the [[GANYMEDE (1979)]] disaster itself, and the later destruction of [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] (which BSSA incorrectly attributed to [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]]), as proof of the protocol's ultimate failure. This belief justifies BSSA's deviation towards more direct methods, including the controlled cultivation of their own RONIN ([[IO (Emerging 1997)]]) and the development of chemical countermeasures like [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)]].
* The ascent of the [[Henry Caraway (Agent Hotel)|Caraway]] faction within [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)]] and its push for consolidation under [[CIA]] oversight represents a significant institutional move away from the protocol's core tenets of decentralization, compartmentalization, and metaphorical obscurity, favoring instead a more centralized and traditionally structured intelligence apparatus.

## Significance in the Campaign
The Mirror-Breaker Protocol is more than just a set of operational guidelines; it's a distinct worldview and a strategy for survival in a cosmos where thought itself can be a battleground. It informs the behavior of allied and neutral factions, provides a framework for understanding certain types of clues and communications, and offers the investigators alternative, non-systematic approaches to the threats they face.
