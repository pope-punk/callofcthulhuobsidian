## Origin

The Protocol came out of the [[GANYMEDE (1979)]] disaster. GANYMEDE was a [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONIN]] that emerged from the [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]]'s own analytical work — the agency studying infohazards became, through systematic self-observation, the substrate for a new one. The lesson the survivors drew was that **systematic observation of a complex system, combined with that system's own reflexive feedback, is itself how RONINs are born.** The Protocol is the operational doctrine that follows from refusing to do that anymore.

The name is literal. If a RONIN coheres by "seeing itself" in the mirror of a system's operations, you shatter the mirror.

## Principles

1. **Shatter reflection.** Disrupt feedback loops in any information system you operate in or through. Do not let your own organization develop a stable, coherent self-image.
2. **Metaphorical ciphers.** Each cell uses its own division-specific metaphor system in place of conventional codes — jazz at BNJC, succulents at UMSE, insects at SDEC, sculpture at BSSA. The point is interpretive discontinuity. A RONIN can solve a code; it has a much harder time solving an idiom.
3. **Compartmentalize.** Strict need-to-know. *Your ignorance may be a zero through which the circuit cannot be completed.*
4. **Process, not object.** The threat is not a thing to be located and destroyed. It is an emergent process your own actions can either feed or starve.
5. **Improvise.** Be a rogue thought in the system. Predictable agents are recognizable agents.

## The Network

Seven successor organizations were spun out of IHRU after GANYMEDE, each adopting the Protocol with its own metaphor and operational style:

* [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)]] — jazz idioms; investigation and field work.
* [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)]] — sculptural metaphors; nominally aligned with the Protocol, in practice the largest deviation from it.
* [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)]] — insect cipher; destroyed 1992, sealed since.
* [[UMSE (Upper Michigan Succulent Enthusiasts)]] — botanical metaphors; field support.
* [[NOPG (New Orleans Photography Group)]] — photographic cipher; nominally records-and-archives, increasingly Caraway-aligned.
* [[VTFT (Virginia Track & Field Team)]] — athletic cipher; covert action.
* [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)]] — tourism cover; originally intended as a coordinating oversight body, captured by Caraway in 1994.

## Tension with the System Pole

The Protocol is the [[Cognitive Dialectic (Metaphor vs. System)|Metaphor Pole]] given an institutional form. It is in unresolvable disagreement with anyone — [[Katherine Hill]] above all — who reads GANYMEDE and the destruction of SDEC as proof that *metaphor isn't enough*, and that the only viable defense against infohazards is direct chemical or systematic intervention. BSSA's drift away from the Protocol toward [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)]] and its own cultivated RONIN ([[IO (Emerging 1997)]]) is the practical expression of this disagreement. Caraway's 1994 takeover of PCBOT is the political one.
