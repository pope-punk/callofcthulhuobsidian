## Professional History

Éric Blanc was the CSIS/SCRS technical contact for the CIA's [[Operation POUTINE]], which used the Trois-Rivières station as its primary location (targeting Canadian Maoists in Quebec City specifically). Shortly before Operation POUTINE concluded in 1986, he resigned over an internal conflict and became a private investigator for a litigation firm on retainer for a French-Canadian commercial insurance company based in [[Montreal, QC]].

After being contacted by [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)]] in 1993 regarding [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]], he traveled to Sequoia Falls to assist in technical forensics, helping dissect radio machinery to try to find the source of [[The Echo]], which both knew had appeared in Axon and Dendrite. Finding the technical instrumentation at the [[Ellensburg Numbers Station]] already heavily compromised by a hasty clean-up operation, the two traveled to the remaining POUTINE stations throughout the US and Canada together to investigate them for signs of activity or re-activation during EUROPA.

Over the course of their investigation, they interviewed a number of persons in the vicinity of Ogunquit, Burlington, Trois-Rivières, Juneau and Duluth, finding that, inexplicably, Juneau, Alaska had also experienced Axon and Dendrite broadcasts from its numbers station. Éric assisted in using anecdotal information about these A&D broadcasts (specifically their signal radius) to reconstruct the signal's propagation mode, as it was not immediately clear how Axon and Dendrite was broadcast live in both locations simultaneously.

## Known Actions & Connections

* **Intelligence Background:** Possessed resources, contacts, and knowledge from his former intelligence career, including technical documentation on transmission systems and access to municipal blueprints and museum records.
* **Alice Redmond Contact:** Connection established through contacts Alice made during her [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]] investigation (identifying the Montreal POUTINE relay). Mentioned by [[Maria Chen]] as a "French Canadian intelligence officer" she met in 1994. His existence as Alice's contact is known to [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nine-String]] within BNJC, but Nine-String never had Blanc's actual address — only a contact channel through Alice.
* **EUROPA Investigation (1993-94):** Assisted Alice in technical forensics related to "Axon and Dendrite" and the [[Ellensburg Numbers Station]]. Their formal collaboration was 'off the books,' and records were later removed from [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)]] archives to protect Blanc's ability to investigate discreetly after the 1994 [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)]] coup. Interviewed [[Chrissy Perkins]] at the [[Special Committee for Artistic Innovation]] but found no initial leads due to records purged by [[Phoebe Jones (Agent Plaster)|Agent Plaster]]. Blanc continued investigating the relay station network unofficially.
* **PCBOT Tunnel Schematics (1994-1996):** During and after the EUROPA investigation, Blanc helped Alice assemble preparatory intelligence in the event Caraway's faction ever needed to be confronted directly. The two obtained 3D schematics of the [[PCBOT Headquarters]] access tunnels via [[Agent Chrysalis]]'s use of the SDEC CICADA-class sonar array (passed to Alice as Mirror-Breaker compartmented contingency material). Blanc stored these and related materials — including a methodology for bypassing PCBOT's internal defenses to obtain remote terminal access to the sealed [[SDEC Facility]] — on the encrypted hard drive at his Montreal apartment. Alice is the only person who knows the passphrase.
* **Located [[Maria Chen]] (Early 1997):** Methodically tracked Maria Chen to Portland via SCAI financial records and equipment purchase orders, keeping her location secret at her request. Chen is the only person outside Alice's compromised channels who knows the address of Blanc's Montreal apartment.
* **Syd Foxglove's Protector (July - October 1997):** Provided sanctuary, support, and arranged access to [[McGill University]] chemistry facilities for [[Syd Foxglove]] from July 17, 1997 onwards. As a trained intelligence officer, Blanc established a **standing emergency meeting protocol** with Syd: if they ever lost contact, Syd was to appear at the [[McCord Museum]] on a designated day each month. Blanc kept these dates circled on the wall calendar in his apartment, with museum admission receipts filed nearby.

## Capture by BSSA (Early October 1997)

Blanc's death was not the result of the [[Steven Rober (Agent Shutter)|Agent Shutter]] leak from BNJC. It resulted from [[Katherine Hill]]'s own investigative work, driven by operational necessity.

* **Investigative Footprint (1994-1997):** Blanc's years of independent inquiry into SCAI records, POUTINE infrastructure, and [[Operation Colossus]] left traces in state and institutional archives. These were noted by BSSA, likely through monitoring mechanisms left in place by Agent Plaster on the records she had purged, or through NOPG channels reviewing old EUROPA case files. Katherine initially flagged Blanc as a low-priority historical nuisance.
* **Alice's Early 1997 Contact:** When Alice began tailing [[Dylan Hill (Agent Destination)]] in early 1997, she contacted Blanc seeking information about [[Operation Colossus]] and [[Katherine Hill]]'s historical activities. Blanc's subsequent inquiries into Katherine's movements (which placed her in Sequoia Falls) elevated his profile from historical nuisance to active concern. Alice told him to back off until hearing from her again.
* **The Parthenon Stall (July - September 1997):** After Syd's escape in July, [[Operation Parthenon]] stalled completely. [[Fauxflake]] refinement required Syd's unique Algorithm aptitude, and Katherine exhausted local avenues searching for him throughout August and September.
* **Connecting the Dots (Late September - Early October):** Desperate to recover Syd, Katherine revisited Alice's operational history through PCBOT/NOPG access. She rediscovered the Blanc flag — a former intelligence contact of Alice's, based in Montreal, who had been actively researching BSSA operations. Montreal was outside BSSA's operational footprint. The logic was clear.
* **Surveillance and Strike (Early October 1997):** Katherine dispatched a team to Montreal. They located Blanc's apartment, broke in while he was at McGill supporting Syd, and ransacked it searching for actionable intelligence. They tapped his phone, cloned his hard drive (recovering the encrypted files but unable to break the encryption without credentials), and identified the email account and the calendar of "appointments" at the [[McCord Museum]]. BSSA then **captured Blanc himself** as he returned, taking him alive to extract his email credentials and his understanding of Syd's daily movements. He is being held at a BSSA black site in or near Montreal as of campaign start.

## The Montreal Trap

With Blanc captured and his credentials extracted under duress, BSSA set a two-pronged trap:

* **The Email Net:** [[Phoebe Jones (Agent Plaster)|Agent Plaster]] now operates Blanc's email account, posing as Blanc to engage anyone who reaches out. Plaster's veteran knowledge of EUROPA-era tradecraft lets her maintain the deception convincingly.
* **The Museum Protocol:** From the calendar evidence at the apartment, BSSA learned of the standing emergency meeting at the [[McCord Museum]] on a designated day each month. Katherine's team prepares to stake out the museum on the next designated date, waiting for Syd to follow the protocol. BSSA does not need to contact Syd directly — they simply need to be at the right place at the right time. (Plaster will also steer any email correspondence with players toward arriving at the museum on the same date.)

## Blanc's Apartment (Investigation Site)

Blanc's apartment is the single Montreal site associated with him — there is no separate safehouse. By the time players reach it, BSSA has already extracted what they could and partially staged the scene to track follow-on visitors.

Players find the apartment **nearly ransacked**: drawers emptied, papers strewn, electronics seized. However, enough evidence remains to piece together the situation:

* **The Calendar:** A wall calendar with specific days circled each month, annotated with cryptic single-letter notes. Cross-referenced with...
* **Museum Receipts:** Filed admission receipts and a museum membership card showing visits to the [[McCord Museum]] on previously circled dates.
* **Personal Effects:** Photographs of Blanc with Alice from the EUROPA period (corroborating his identity), and items suggesting he has not been home in days.
* **Signs of Capture, Not Death:** Spilled coffee, an overturned chair near the door, partial signs of a brief struggle — but no body, no blood pattern consistent with murder on site.
* **The Computer (Critical):** Blanc's personal computer remains. Its encrypted hard drive holds the **PCBOT tunnel schematics** and the **methodology to bypass PCBOT internal defenses to obtain remote terminal access to the sealed [[SDEC Facility]]** — both passed to Blanc by Alice during the 1994–1996 period. The disk is password-protected; only Alice knows the passphrase. Some of the same files were copied off by BSSA but remain encrypted in their hands as well.

This combination — Blanc gone, the apartment hit, the calendar pointing to the museum on a specific day, the locked computer requiring Alice — is what makes curing Alice a hard requirement before the players can proceed to PCBOT.

## Status

**Captured, alive (for now).** Held by BSSA at an undisclosed location in or near Montreal. His email account is being operated by [[Phoebe Jones (Agent Plaster)|Agent Plaster]]. He is most likely to die during the shootout that follows whatever rescue or capture attempt occurs at the [[McCord Museum]] (or at a BSSA holding site if the players track him there). His apartment hard drive contains the encrypted PCBOT-bypass material that only Alice can decode.
