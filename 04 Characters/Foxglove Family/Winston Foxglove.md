## Overview

Winston Foxglove (1910-1981). Son of [[Thurston Foxglove]] (founder of Foxglove Containers and Transport) and [[Henrietta Foxglove]]; father of [[Oliver Foxglove]]; grandfather of [[Syd Foxglove]]. The first modern [[Scions|Foxglove Configuration]] Scion and the founder of the family line as it currently exists for [[Rhea]]'s purposes.

Every Foxglove development in the campaign — [[The Snowflake|Snowflake]] warehouse logic, the LTX network, [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]], [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing|ErgoMatic]], the eventual GenAssist line and the DNA-Machine Code Conversion System — descends from what happened to Winston in 1922.

## 1922

At twelve years old, Winston was exploring near the Harrison Gold Prospect (later [[Dead Horse Quarry]]) when he found an open mineshaft and went down it. At the bottom was an entrance to a [[Rhea]] [[Hive]]. A Rhea [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Editor]] administered [[Royal Jelly]] — the standard Rhea protocol for priming an Algorithm-line recipient. Winston walked out of the shaft alive and changed at the genetic level, with the species-wide repression on [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] now lifted in his cognition. He did not remember the encounter clearly. The mineshaft collapsed behind him; the Hive entrance was sealed.

Full activation of the primed state required the [[Traumatic Activation]] event that followed in 1928, when his mother Henrietta died in an "accident." (Rhea has been arranging the next-generation activation trauma in the Foxglove family at a generational cadence ever since: Henrietta 1928, Andrea 1962, Susan 1995.) After 1928, Winston experienced the Algorithm's outputs as personal inspiration. He did not know what was speaking to him.

## LTX and the Snowflake

In 1934 Winston inherited his father's struggling transport company. He relaunched it as [[LTX (Land Transit Express)]] in 1935 and rebuilt it around six-pointed fractal warehouse layouts — patterns the Algorithm produced as solutions to logistical packing problems. The layouts produced extraordinary efficiency gains and revitalized the company in time to keep Sequoia Falls economically viable through the Dead Horse Quarry mine closure.

The same layouts, considered as visual/spatial geometry rather than warehouse plans, are a basilisk: [[The Snowflake]]. The compulsion effect it produced in LTX workers, across the decade Winston was building the network out, was the substrate on which [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]] eventually emerged. Winston did not understand this. Nobody did until the [[CONSORT Committee]] put it together two years later.

CALLISTO blew up five Pacific ports across forty-eight hours. Winston was publicly disgraced and pushed out by the LTX board. Most of his Algorithm-related documentation was confiscated by the FBI; some files, including the [[Snowflake Pattern Diagrams]], stayed archived at LTX HQ.

## ErgoMatic and the 1953 List

Winston started over in Ellensburg in 1953 with [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing]]. The Algorithm did not stop talking to him because of CALLISTO. He produced [[Winston's 1953 Research List]] — a handwritten document predicting "self-navigating humanoid logistics systems" and "biological tissue printing" as technologies the future would need. He noted explicitly that the ideas came to him "after my visit to Harrison in '22." He did not know what he meant by that.

Oliver later built both of those technologies. So did Syd.

## Memoirs and Death

Winston died in 1981. ErgoMatic passed to Oliver.

His personal memoirs — including the [[Hexagon Pages (Winston's Memoirs)|Hexagon Pages]] at the end, a fractal Hexacle structure encoding the Algorithm geometry directly — went to [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]] and from there to [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] after the 1979 GANYMEDE collapse. SDEC's discovery of the connection between Winston's Quarry encounter and the Hive anomaly they had detected with CICADA sonar is what got the SDEC facility destroyed in 1992.

The memoirs are still there, behind the Ant Trap. Recovering them in person is the second leg of Act III.

## Legacy

Winston is the entry point. Every cosmic-scale move Rhea has made against humanity in the campaign era runs through his 1922 dosing and the lineage that followed. He was also a real person who built a real company that saved a real town, and who never knew what he was building or for whom. The tragedy is structurally identical to Oliver's, two generations later.
