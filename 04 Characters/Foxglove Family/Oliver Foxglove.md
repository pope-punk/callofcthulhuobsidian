## Overview

Oliver Foxglove (1947-1997). Son of [[Winston Foxglove]] and [[Andrea Foxglove]]; father of [[Syd Foxglove]]. CEO of [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing]] from 1981 until his murder on January 10, 1997.

Oliver inherited the [[Scions|Foxglove Configuration]] — Algorithm access from Winston's [[Royal Jelly]] lineage, activated when Andrea died in 1962 in a Rhea-orchestrated "accident." Where Winston wielded the Algorithm consciously and saw it as inspiration, Oliver's relationship with it was largely subconscious. The Algorithm steered his research and development priorities at ErgoMatic without his realizing it was steering them. He built what [[Rhea]] needed.

## The work

Under Oliver's direction, ErgoMatic established two new divisions whose research lines — both intuitively driven, both nominally his ideas — happened to produce exactly the technologies Rhea required for its substrate transition:

* **EAS (Robotics/Automation)** — the development line that became [[Signal Dummies]].
* **BAD (Biomedical)** — tissue engineering, including Syd's later [[GenAssist Universal Tissue Printer]], and the [[DNA-Machine Code Conversion System]].

Oliver framed all of this as Foxglove-family rehabilitation work. The family name had been ruined by [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]] and Winston's ouster from LTX; Oliver wanted it back. He thought the LTX-ErgoMatic merger — which he initiated in 1995 and championed through 1996 — would be the public vindication. He was wrong about what he was actually building. He was right about whose hands were on the wheel.

## December 1996

Oliver's realization came not from a dusty document but from looking at his son's notes. Visiting Syd's workstation at ErgoMatic, Oliver noticed Syd's idle marginalia included recurring symbols he had only ever seen in two places before: Winston's private notebooks, and his own rare scratch-pad notes during deep Algorithm reverie. [[Ylynthii Numerals]], spontaneously produced by Syd, with no exposure, no instruction, no source.

Genetic. Not learned. Inherited.

Working backward from that, Oliver re-examined his own active projects — GenAssist, the DNA-Machine Code Conversion System — and found that under analysis they were spontaneously replicating the structural skeleton of his father's algorithm. He had not designed them this way consciously. The Algorithm was running through him to produce its own substrate, and the LTX-ErgoMatic merger was the linchpin.

He moved to halt the merger.

He also tried to contact [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] — the "bug people" his father's papers had referenced — and got no response. SDEC had been destroyed in 1992 without his knowledge.

## Final weeks

In the panicked period between his discovery and his death, Oliver:

* Pulled his sensitive bio-integration research off ErgoMatic's servers and locked it in his home safe.
* Stored alongside it a vacuum-sealed sample of an ErgoMatic-internal reagent ([[THEBES]]) — the precursor for any non-gallium F7 antidote.
* Set the safe code to his mother Andrea's birthday, encoded through his own [[Oliver Foxglove's Cipher Notes|Ylynthii-base cipher]]. He chose the cipher knowing Syd, and only Syd, could intuit it.
* Wrote a series of panicked emails to Syd ([[Oliver Foxglove Emails]]) pointing at the safe, at Andrea's birthday, at Winston's memoirs, at SDEC, at the merger.

On **January 10, 1997**, hours before a scheduled meeting with lawyers to formally postpone the merger, Oliver was killed by a [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Reaper]] in his home. Syd witnessed it.

## The cover-up

The Sheriff's deputy who responded was [[Bruce Kennedy]] of the [[Hammerskull Crew]]. Kennedy's first move was to frame Syd. [[Katherine Hill]] arrived in time to stop the frame-up — not out of charity, but because she'd already noticed Syd's atypical reaction profile during preliminary observation, and recognized a more valuable use for him at [[Operation Parthenon]].

Syd's testimony about a non-human assailant was filed by BSSA as infohazardous exposure — possibly Echo-like contamination — and used to justify involuntary "treatment" at [[Saint Anselm's Center for Hope]] and later [[Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life]]. No real investigation of Oliver's death ever happened. The merger closed on January 15.

## Legacy

Oliver is the only Foxglove who built the substrate Rhea wanted *and* tried to stop the substrate from being delivered. The contents of his safe are the campaign's chemical key — without them, no G6, no cure for Alice, no path through PCBOT and SDEC. The cipher he built around the safe is also a kind of message: only Syd can open it. He left Syd the means and the responsibility in the same gesture.
