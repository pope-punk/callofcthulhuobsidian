## Description

Edwin Burberry is the wealthy, somewhat eccentric, Connecticut-based founder and owner of **[[American Meridian]]**, the capital finance firm that held the majority share in [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]].

## Known Actions & Connections

* **[[American Meridian]]**: Founder and owner. His firm viewed LTX primarily through a financial lens.
* **LTX Management**: Maintained the unprofitable Sequoia Falls LTX branch as a consistent tax write-off against American Meridian's other profitable ventures (like rail infrastructure).
* **LTX-ErgoMatic Merger**: Saw the merger solely as an opportunity for profit consolidation and further tax advantages. Appointed [[Richard Harmon]] as "Transitional CEO" of LTX specifically to push the merger through, overriding any local operational concerns.
* **Role in Rhea's Plan**: His detached, purely financial perspective and decisions inadvertently served [[Rhea]]'s goals by facilitating the crucial LTX-[[ErgoMatic Manufacturing|ErgoMatic]] merger, creating the substrate Rhea needed for its technological transcendence.
* He's a red herring.

## Status

Presumed living as of 1997, likely entirely unaware of the cosmic horror his financial decisions helped unleash.
