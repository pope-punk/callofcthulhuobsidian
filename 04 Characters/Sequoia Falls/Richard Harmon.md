## Description

Richard Harmon served as the "Transitional CEO" of [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]] from mid-1996 through the completion of the [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing|ErgoMatic]] merger in January 1997.

## Known Actions & Connections

* **Appointment**: Specifically installed as CEO by [[American Meridian]] (the majority shareholder, owned by [[Edwin Burberry]]) to push through the financially advantageous merger with ErgoMatic. He replaced the previous CEO, Martin Kavanaugh.
* **Role in Merger**: Implemented financially-driven restructuring at LTX to facilitate the merger, overriding local operational concerns or the strategic objections raised by [[Oliver Foxglove]]. His focus was solely on executing American Meridian's directive for profit consolidation.
* **Rhea's Plan**: Like American Meridian, Harmon inadvertently served [[Rhea]]'s goals by ensuring the merger proceeded, creating the integrated LTX-ErgoMatic substrate.

## Status

His status after the merger completed in January 1997 is not detailed. He successfully fulfilled his role as transitional CEO.
