## Overview

Deacon Anselm Chambers — formerly Andrew Chamberlain — is BSSA's principal operational hand in Sequoia Falls. He runs the two BSSA front sites: [[Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life]] (and the [[Operation Parthenon|Parthenon]] chamber beneath it) and [[Saint Anselm's Center for Hope]] (and the [[Project Ozymandias|Ozymandias]] labs beneath it). He answers to [[Katherine Hill]] and reports through her PCBOT/BSSA channel.

His career-defining skill is applied infohazard work — taking [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] outputs and shaping them into patterns that do specific things to specific people. He has been doing this longer than BSSA has officially employed him.

## Polybius

In 1978 Chamberlain — then an LTX employee — found archived documentation on [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] in the company files and recognized what he was looking at. He resigned, founded Sierra Entertainment as cover, and in the early 1980s built an arcade game called [[Polybius]] using Algorithm principles to produce specific psychological effects in players. The Seattle-area cult that formed around the game in 1983-84 was Chamberlain's first private experiment in cultivating a guided audience around an infohazard.

In 1984, BSSA raided the cult. The raid was the cover for what actually happened: Katherine Hill (Agent Caryatid at the time) and [[Henry Caraway (Agent Hotel)|Henry Caraway]] (then a PCBOT agent) had identified Chamberlain as someone BSSA wanted operationally and arranged for him to fake his death during the raid. He resurfaced as Deacon Anselm Chambers, a religious-front asset with full BSSA backing and the institutional resources he had previously been improvising without.

## Operational record

* **[[Operation Colossus]] (1986–1992).** Founded the [[Chapel of the Blessed Lamb]] in Ellensburg. Ran the "Seventh Hour" radio program as the early testing platform for [[The Echo]]. Recruited [[Jacob Wise]] and [[Erin Cross]] (both former Polybius cultists) as lieutenants. Operations collapsed when SDEC disrupted EUROPA in 1992.
* **Sequoia Falls (1995–present).** Relaunched the Ellensburg work as the Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life. Designed the [[Fauxflake]] pattern derivative of [[The Snowflake]] — most of the conceptual work is his, drawing on Polybius. Built and now manages the underground chamber where IO is being cultivated. Concurrent: established Saint Anselm's as the homeless-shelter front for Ozymandias.
* **[[Syd Foxglove]] coercion (Jan–Jul 1997).** When Fauxflake refinement stalled in 1996, Chambers and Katherine identified [[The Foxglove Family|Foxglove]] cognition as the bottleneck. After Katherine took Syd into BSSA custody, Chambers personally oversaw Syd's coerced refinement work in the Seventh Tabernacle chamber. Fauxflake's efficacy improved measurably; the work stopped only when Syd escaped in July.

## Status (late October 1997)

Active and central to both Parthenon and Ozymandias. The persona is the religious-figure cover — calm, charismatic, low-affect, somewhat reassuring on the surface. The competence underneath is real. Chambers genuinely believes BSSA's project, in the same way a research scientist believes their dissertation. He is also a man who once built a video game specifically to put people into infohazardous trance states and watched what happened. The public-facing softness is not an accident; it is the work product of someone who has spent twenty years studying how to manipulate the room he is in.

In play, Chambers is the most likely BSSA antagonist the players will encounter before the McCord operation. The Seventh Tabernacle revisit, the Saint Anselm's infiltration, and the Fauxflake-chamber investigation all run through him.
