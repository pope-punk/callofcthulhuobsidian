## Overview
Alice Redmond, known within the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol|Mirror-Breaker network]] as Agent Altissimo, is a former FBI agent renowned for her intelligence and resourcefulness. Recruited into the [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)|Blue Note Jazz Collective]] around 1990, she was a promising and effective operative, making significant progress on investigating the [[EUROPA (1986-1992)]] event and its aftermath throughout the mid-1990s until her investigation was shuttered by [[PCBOT (Pacific Coast Board of Tourism)|PCBOT]]. No longer able to continue her collaboration with [[Éric Blanc]] on EUROPA, she turned her investigation toward Sequoia Falls and LTX, forming a friendship with [[Syd Foxglove]]. This connection helped the Collective circumvent the bureaucratic roadblocks established by PCBOT after 1994 and access the company's records.

Following Syd's disappearance in early 1997, Alice (in collaboration with Director Nine-String) began investigating [[Dylan Hill (Agent Destination)]], based on a tip from the deposed Jacqui Lopez that his wife, Katherine, was active in Sequoia Falls. Tailing Hill through the late spring and early summer of 1997 led to her discovery of an apparent collaborative network between Saint Anselm's, Seventh Tabernacle, and the Hammerskulls. Before she could prepare a formal report, she saw Dylan transferring the kidnapped Syd from [[Saint Anselm's Center for Hope]] back toward [[Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life]]. After intercepting Hill and freeing Syd, she interrogated Dylan. During his failed escape attempt, he managed to stab her with a full syringe of Fluoroxepram, resulting in a massive overdose of the experimental chemical [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]], which left her cognitively impaired and dangerously unpredictable. As of late October 1997 Alice operates as a rogue agent, driven by a fractured perception of reality, hunting what she believes to be components of a new and insidious RONIN at LTX, which are in fact [[Rhea]]'s [[Signal Dummies]].

## Early Life
Alice was born in 1966 to Clintock and Ondaline Redmond in Los Angeles, California. The child of an unlikely marriage between a disaffected Marxist-Leninist activist (Ondaline) and a disillusioned FBI agent (Clintock), Alice became the new locus of idealism for both formerly ideologically-motivated parents.

Consequently, she grew up under significant pressure to please her parents, both of whom now saw her, rather than communism or patriotism, as their future. With significant financial resources available on both sides of the family tree, they saw to it that their daughter's talents were nurtured: extensive music lessons, daily tutoring in advanced mathematics and Greek philosophy, equestrianism, swimming, and shooting.

This left her with little time for normal social development, a tension that persisted through her enrollment at Berkeley in 1983 — having just turned 17, she was at least a full year younger than her freshman classmates.

In turn, she relied on connections from her parents' Ivy-league backgrounds for social connection, which intersected precisely in criminology (her mother having gone into the legal field as a prosecutor). Her friend group consisted of professors (a combination of impressed academics and her parents' professional contacts) and a circle of computer hackers, who respected her mathematical acumen. This positioned her well to become a prime candidate for FBI recruitment when she graduated early in 1986 — socially unsavvy in most ways but on the cutting edge of mathematics and technology.

With J. Edgar Hoover dead and buried, and both parents aglow in the neoliberal optimism of the 1980s, they gave their blessing for Alice to bring the Redmond name back to the FBI's roster.

## FBI Career
Alice completed her training at Quantico on the same week she turned 21. Having acquired a reputation as a consummate wunderkind, she was quickly attached as an investigator to an active case at the FBI's San Francisco field office. Her career began with distinction: this assignment culminated in her capture of the notorious [[Edmund White|Balloon Mask Killer]], whose reign of terror spanned the entire West Coast and ended at [[Edmund White Estate|Edmund White's vacation property]] southwest of Route 90. This success — and particularly Alice's use of statistical methodologies — brought her to the attention of the Mirror-Breaker network, leading to her recruitment into the BNJC (then located in Sacramento) around 1990. At around this time, she began renting a house in Sequoia Falls, which she would later buy in 1993.

## BNJC Sacramento
Redmond began her 14-month Mirror-Breaker training course in July of 1990 and concluded in September of 1991. In addition to rigorous training in the metaphorical cipher of the Collective, she also completed a curriculum based on that of the IHRU. It was around this time that she became close to [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nigel Christmas]] — she struggled to form friendships with the other agents her age (particularly Agent Legato, a few years older than her), and instead lapsed into her established pattern of bonding with authority figures over peers. For his part, Christmas came to see her as a daughter; her wild talent signified, in some way, the future of the organization.

Her Sacramento casework largely revolved around ensuring that upper West Coast tech firms were observing randomization protocols. Until the end of 1992, she conducted regular site visits to IBM, Pacific Telesis, and others. During this period, she identified and helped pre-empt a possible non-Jovian RONIN (no verified presence of the Foxglove Algorithm), termed Cadence 11, forming in an unregulated telephone subsystem at Pacific Telesis.

This victory was seen as a win for the traditionalist approach to Mirror-Breaking, but came at the cost of antagonizing the Collective's rivals. Compliance with randomization protocols was difficult and expensive for rapidly expanding tech companies, and the notion of RONIN without clear connection to the Foxglove algorithm had long been tendentious. Well-connected Silicon Valley moguls pressed their contacts in the CIA and State Department to remove some randomization regulations, a request ultimately granted in 1994.

## EUROPA Investigation
Following the [[EUROPA (1986-1992)|EUROPA]] event and the subsequent factional struggle, BNJC relocated from Sacramento to Sequoia Falls to continue the SDEC-initiated investigation into the Echo. Audio analysis of recorded Axon and Dendrite episodes revealed an apparent feedback pattern between voice pitch and a background tone played during the call-in section. The background tone caused distinct fear and disorientation in BNJC researchers, strongly suggesting a Basilisk. While other analysts (primary among them Agent Chrysalis) turned their attention back toward the Foxgloves and LTX, Alice turned toward field work. Using Operation POUTINE records, she began investigating the numbers stations, beginning with the one in Ellensburg. Finding it stripped, she turned to other known POUTINE locations and soon met [[Éric Blanc]] via her FBI connections.

The two bonded quickly and spent much of early 1993 traveling the POUTINE network. Of the six numbers stations visited, only Juneau, Alaska had broadcast Axon and Dendrite (1991 to 1992).

During this time, the two became romantically entangled. Alice became very attached to the older Canadian, who did his best to manage her expectations. Nonetheless, the two unofficially dated for much of late 1993 and 1994.

Unable to find actionable evidence in Alaska, the pair returned to Sequoia Falls to re-examine the SCAI financials. However, shortly after interviewing [[Chrissy Perkins]] in late 1994, the EUROPA investigation was suddenly and officially transferred to NOPG as part of the changes following Caraway's coup. At Nine-String's direction, Alice discontinued her collaboration with Blanc but encouraged him to unofficially continue the investigation, which he did. The two "broke up" as a result of the work ending; hard feelings, particularly on Alice's part, kept contact limited.

### The PCBOT Contingency File (1994-1996)
During and after the official end of the EUROPA partnership, Alice and Blanc quietly assembled a personal contingency dossier in case Caraway's faction ever needed to be confronted directly. Using [[Agent Chrysalis]]'s SDEC CICADA-class sonar readings, the two acquired 3D facility schematics of [[PCBOT Headquarters]]. Alice also brought to Blanc something she had been carrying since well before the coup: copies of the [[SDEC Ant Trap Protocol Documents]] she had quietly stolen from PCBOT's secure archives during the EUROPA investigation. She had originally taken them with a half-formed intention of opening SDEC herself — Caraway's takeover put a stop to that plan before she had to confront whether she would have actually done it. With Blanc, she instead worked the documents and the schematics into a single override procedure: how a small team could reach PCBOT's control center and remotely disengage SDEC's physical Ant Trap from a continent away. Alice encrypted the entire package and gave it to Blanc to store on the hard drive at his Montreal apartment. **Alice is the only person who knows the passphrase**, and she is the only person fluent enough in the bespoke mix of stolen documentation, SDEC sonar logic, EUROPA-era tradecraft, and PCBOT internal layout to actually execute the procedure end-to-end. This file is the campaign's true path into PCBOT and onward to SDEC — its existence is unknown to BSSA, PCBOT, BNJC, and even Syd until the players surface it.

## Sequoia Falls Stalemate
After Caraway's coup, all corporate records requests had to be routed through a PCBOT liaison. This enormously complicated BNJC's operational tasks. With EUROPA records officially transferred to NOPG, and LTX data filtered through Dylan Hill, BNJC was organizationally hamstrung.

Taking a by-then-characteristic investigative approach, Alice leveraged Agent Chrysalis' contacts with the Foxglove family (carried over from his time at SDEC) to connect with Syd, mostly unbeknownst to the rest of BNJC (and especially unknown to Hill). Throughout mid-late '95 and '96, Syd helped Alice access LTX data without filtering, which they surreptitiously transferred to their analyst team. Neither line of investigation proved fruitful, but they continued to cross-check Hill's LTX data with Syd's as a matter of course.

Outside of work, Alice spent time with Syd and his girlfriend Amanda Green. Alice and Syd formed a quick friendship, having in common a love of music and the strenuous social and parental expectations that came with their respective exceptional talents.

## Syd's Disappearance and Alice's Investigation
The events of January 1997 had a profound impact on Alice. Her only real friend had disappeared as the sole witness to a high-profile murder. Although she had never met [[Oliver Foxglove]], Alice immediately knew that Syd could not have been responsible for his death, and she began inquiries into the Kittitas County case files, which she was initially denied, then granted in what she correctly deduced to be doctored form.

Powerless to intervene directly, she began tailing Dylan Hill throughout the first half of 1997, with Nine-String's blessing — a blessing secured by a tip he received from Jacqui Lopez that Katherine Hill was reportedly active in Sequoia Falls. Over the following months, she documented his movements, observing him traveling from BNJC to the White Stag, the Center for Hope, the Seventh Tabernacle Church of Life, The Conifers, and on I-90.

During this period, Alice also re-engaged Blanc to research [[Operation Colossus]] and Katherine Hill's historical activity. Blanc's resulting inquiries elevated his profile from "historical nuisance" to "active concern" with Katherine — the seed of the eventual BSSA operation that would capture him in early October.

## The Turning Point: July 1997
In early July, Dylan informed the Collective he'd be returning to Washington, DC. His final visit to the collective occurred on July 13th. On July 15th, Alice was following him once again, and this proved decisive: this was the day Dylan had been tasked with returning Syd from the Center for Hope back to the Church of Life. Alice ambushed Agent Destination on north I-90.

Recognizing that Syd was not safe, she dropped him off at the bus stop near the I-90 overpass with instructions to head to Montreal and meet with Éric Blanc until she could contact him. She then took Dylan to the abandoned [[Edmund White Estate]] and interrogated him. Dylan refused to give answers beyond "his detention is related to a PCBOT investigation of LTX" unless he could speak to Director Hotel first. Alice escalated toward torture. Dylan slipped a hand out of a handcuff and a struggle ensued, during which he injected her with a fluoroxepram syringe. However, due to her prescription adderall and her Mirror-Breaking training, she avoided immediate incapacitation and managed to kill Hill, bludgeoning him to death with her saxophone.

## The F7 Fugitive: A War Against Shadows
As her systematizing capacity buckled under the chemical pressure, Alice began forming associative lateral connections, putting together an incomplete picture of a RONIN at LTX which had been somehow concealed by Hill. She traveled toward I-90 and entered the Center of the Universe, following the Hexacles which were now visible to her. She interpreted the emergence of these strange shapes as some kind of "guide", indicating lines of flight beyond the logic of roads and intersections.

She met a number of COTU residents but obtained limited information about the Hexacles. She was told they led to a tunnel and a guardhouse where the Rubber Man lived. This was Thermos Milkshaft, the first Signal Dummy Alice would encounter and destroy. It resisted small arms fire from her service weapon and she ultimately handcuffed it to the desk in the chamber before returning to her vehicle. She made a trip home, gathered the last of her adderall and a satellite phone, but was unable to access her safe — her F7-addled mind could no longer parse the concept of a code. She was also unable to call the BNJC for similar reasons. She returned to COTU, where a less-impaired resident helped her utilize the satellite phone to contact BNJC; she was unable to communicate information beyond her location and her need to continue her anti-RONIN operations outside official oversight. She coordinated a first meeting with Agent Chrysalis at I-90 to obtain food and ammunition. She also used the satellite phone to contact Éric Blanc and confirm Syd had arrived in Montreal, and obtained a burner phone, the number of which she wrote down with assistance.

From July to September 1997, Alice, operating from the [[Edmund White Estate]], used Dylan's manifests to hunt and destroy numerous [[Signal Dummies]], believing them to be components of a new, dangerous RONIN. Her compromised state led her to tragically kill LTX employees [[Sarah Prescott]] (August 10th) and [[Thomas Reeves]] (September 8th), mistaking them for Dummies. Throughout this period, she maintained covert contact with [[Agent Chrysalis]], who facilitated secure communications with [[Syd Foxglove]] as he began G6 research.

A [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Reaper]] attack at the White estate on September 8th forced her to relocate her base of operations to the [[Sewers and Maintenance Tunnels|tunnels]] beneath the [[COTU (Center of the Universe)|Center of the Universe (COTU)]] encampment. [[Rhea]] then appeared to target her further via [[Calvin Pierce]]'s distribution of pure F7 at COTU in late September, which led to mass casualties. Alice, discovering the devastation and Calvin Pierce's subsequent murder by a [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Writer]], retreated deeper into the tunnels. Her killing of [[Sean Fielding]] around this time was another tragic consequence of her F7-induced misidentification.

## Current Status and Mental State (Late October 1997)
As of late October 1997, Alice is a rogue agent hiding in the sewers and maintenance tunnels near [[COTU (Center of the Universe)]]. She is severely impaired by the F7 overdose, her perception of reality distorted, and her mission to destroy the "LTX RONIN" all-consuming. She killed [[Pastor Mike]] at the [[Redwood Shopping Mall]] on October 7th, dropped her satchel during her escape, and is in desperate need of the [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] antidote Syd is trying to develop.

### Her interpretive frame

Alice's working theory is the one she had been building **before** the F7 injection: that [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)|BSSA]] has built or is incubating a weaponized RONIN whose material substrate runs through the LTX shipping network. F7-impairment forecloses her capacity to revise that frame. Everything she encounters is now sorted by whether it fits:

* **[[Signal Dummies]] → fit.** They are mechanical, networked, dirt-shipment-linked, and exactly what an LTX-routed RONIN substrate would look like. She hunts them.
* **[[The Hexacle]] → fit, repurposed.** Pre-F7 these patterns were Rhea's amnesia-inducing perimeter marker. Post-F7 their amnesia mechanism is dampened (the same mechanism that drew COTU's residents to the perimeter is operating on her), so she perceives them instead as a navigational system — "lines of flight beyond the logic of roads." She follows them. They led her to COTU and to [[Signal Dummies|Thermos Milkshaft]].
* **[[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Reapers, Writers, Watchers, Spectator Grubs]] → do not fit.** Biomorphic predators have no place in her LTX-RONIN model. Her broken pattern recognition therefore categorizes them as **basilisk-induced or F7-induced perceptual hallucinations**. She flees them rather than engaging them as real adversaries. This is why the Sept 8 Reaper attack at the [[Edmund White Estate]] reads as a retreat rather than a fight, and why she has never tried to study or hunt Rhea's biological layer.

The consequence is that Alice is fighting one operational tier of Rhea's local infrastructure (the Dummies) while a deeper tier (Rhea's actual agents) moves through the same space unopposed. She cannot conceive of [[Rhea]] as an entity. She is killing automatons in the belief that she is dismantling BSSA's worst weapon, and she is treating the actual predator network as if it were inside her own head.

## Future Role: Required Checkpoint, Redemption, and Sacrifice

Curing Alice is no longer an incidental humanitarian goal — it is the **operational gate** to the PCBOT and SDEC arcs. The override procedure that gets the team into Caraway's facility and remotely disengages the [[SDEC Facility|SDEC]] Ant Trap lives on the encrypted hard drive at Blanc's apartment. **Alice is the only person who knows the passphrase** and the only person who can practicably execute the procedure end-to-end. Until she is restored:

* The drive cannot be decrypted.
* The PCBOT operation cannot be planned.
* SDEC cannot be reached.

This makes the **Montreal → Rescue Syd → Synthesize G6 → Cure Alice → Decrypt Drive → PCBOT → SDEC** chain structurally necessary.

Once cured, Alice:

1. Confirms [[Reapers|Reapers]]/[[Writers|Writers]] reality and the basic shape of the conspiracy.
2. Recalls [[Agent Chrysalis]]'s flight after COTU and what little she knows of his fallback intent (Chrysalis is no longer at PCBOT in this canon; he has gone to ground somewhere outside Sequoia Falls).
3. **Decrypts the Blanc drive** and walks the team through the override — she put most of the schematic and procedural intelligence on that drive herself, working with Blanc and Chrysalis between 1994 and 1996, and the [[SDEC Ant Trap Protocol Documents]] at the heart of it she had quietly carried out of PCBOT years earlier.
4. **Accompanies the team to Washington D.C.** to execute the operation in person. She has been inside PCBOT HQ before (during the 1993-94 EUROPA period, before Caraway's coup compartmentalized her out) and she retains live familiarity with its internal layout that no one else on the team has. And — past the operational logic — she wants to go. Blanc is dead, or close to it, and the people who killed him are at the other end of the override.

During the climax of the PCBOT operation, as Caraway initiates the "[[PCBOT Headquarters|Departing Flight]]" airstrike protocol against Sequoia Falls, the investigators will also deduce that enhanced pattern recognition is needed to decrypt the strike frequencies. Alice, understanding their plan, is expected to volunteer for chemical decryption.

Her fate then diverges based on the chemical used:

* **Pathway 1 (G5):** If she uses PCBOT's [[G5 (Gallium-stabilized Amphetamine Complex)|G5]], she successfully decrypts the codes, allowing [[Nigel Christmas (Nine-String)|Nine-String]] to jam the airstrike with the [[BNJC Radiometry Array]]. She escapes with the team but succumbs to fatal gallium poisoning during the journey back to the West Coast, allowing for final moments of clarity.
* **Pathway 2 (G6):** If she uses the players' [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]], she again enables the jamming but then enters the passive, automaton-like state associated with [[Rhea]]'s "Induced Susceptibility." She becomes unreachable and cannot be convinced to leave the facility.
* **Pathway 3 (G6 + F7):** If she takes G6, enables jamming, and enters the automaton state, a subsequent dose of F7 might temporarily revert her to baseline lucidity, allowing her to escape with the team. However, this volatile G6/F7 interaction would prove fatal.

Alice's journey is one from dedicated agent to impaired hunter, and potentially to a figure of tragic sacrifice — her cognition the literal key the team relies on, and her destruction the price of using it.
