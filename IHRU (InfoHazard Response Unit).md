## Overview

The **InfoHazard Response Unit** was the US government's original organization for investigating [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONINs]] and information hazards. Founded by [[Hannah Christmas]] in 1951 on the recommendation of the [[CONSORT Committee]] after the [[CALLISTO (1949)]] event. Dissolved in 1979.

## What killed it

IHRU's working methodology was straightforwardly analytical: collect the data on the previous RONIN event, study it systematically, learn from it. The data they were studying was the CALLISTO data. The analytical pipeline was running on ARPANET.

On **July 5, 1979**, the systematic analysis of pattern data produced — inside the network running the analysis — the [[Black Coral]] basilisk, which the analysts had inadvertently generated by visualizing it. Exposure caused violent psychosis and a compulsion to reproduce the pattern by hand. The IHRU facility was overrun. [[Hannah Christmas]] and dozens of personnel were killed. ARPANET was taken down. The facility itself was destroyed in an airstrike.

GANYMEDE was the moment the analytical approach proved suicidal.

## Legacy

IHRU's dissolution produced the seven [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol|Mirror-Breaker cells]] and the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol|Protocol]] itself, which is a direct doctrinal response to GANYMEDE. The cell network was deliberately decentralized, deliberately metaphorical, deliberately resistant to the kind of systematic self-observation that produced Black Coral.

Surviving IHRU personnel and records went to the new cells. [[Winston Foxglove]]'s memoirs went to [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] — which set up SDEC for what would happen to *it* in 1992.
