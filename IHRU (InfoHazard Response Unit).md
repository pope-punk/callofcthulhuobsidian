## Overview
The original US government organization founded to investigate [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)]] and related information hazards. Predecessor to the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol|Mirror-Breaker network]].

## History
* **Founding (1951):** Established by [[Hannah Christmas]] following the [[CALLISTO (1949)]] event and the recommendations of the [[CONSORT Committee]].
* **Methodology:** Likely employed more traditional, systematic analytical methods to investigate infohazards, which ultimately proved dangerous.
* **GANYMEDE Disaster (July 5, 1979):** While analyzing CALLISTO data on ARPANET, IHRU researchers inadvertently created the [[Black Coral]] basilisk, triggering the [[GANYMEDE (1979)]] RONIN event. The basilisk caused violent insanity. The IHRU facility was compromised, [[Hannah Christmas]] and dozens of personnel were killed. The facility was destroyed in an airstrike, and ARPANET was temporarily taken down.
* **Legacy:** The GANYMEDE disaster proved the danger of systematic analysis and led directly to the dissolution of IHRU and the creation of the decentralized [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol|Mirror-Breaker network]] operating under the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol]]. Surviving personnel and records were distributed among the new agencies (e.g., [[Winston Foxglove]]'s memoirs went to [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)]]).

## Status
* Defunct since 1979.
