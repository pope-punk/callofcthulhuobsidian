## Overview
A visual/spatial [[Basilisks & Infohazards|basilisk]] derived directly from [[The Foxglove Algorithm]]. Implemented by [[Winston Foxglove]] in the 1930s as highly efficient warehouse layouts for [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]]. Served as the immanent vector for the [[CALLISTO (1949)]] RONIN.

## Appearance
* A **six-pointed fractal pattern** used as the architectural basis for warehouse floor plans and operational layouts. Visible primarily from above or through analysis of operational data/blueprints.

## Mechanism & Effect
* **Targets Algorithm Vulnerability:** Exploits cognitive pathways linked to [[The Foxglove Algorithm]].
* **Passive Compulsion:** Induced a subtle "passive compulsion" effect in LTX workers interacting with the pattern on a systemic level. This didn't cause direct madness but influenced behavior across the network, facilitating coordinated action and creating the feedback loops necessary for [[CALLISTO (1949)]]'s emergence. Workers might feel unusually content or driven while working within the pattern.

## History & Significance
* Designed by [[Winston Foxglove]] based on his conscious Algorithm access. Credited with LTX's revitalization.
* Identified by the [[CONSORT Committee]] as the **immanent vector** for [[CALLISTO (1949)]]. Its analysis led to the RONIN theory.
* Original documentation ([[Snowflake Pattern Diagrams]]) is sought by [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)]] (via [[Sean Fielding]]) to aid their [[Fauxflake]] research after Syd's escape.
* [[BSSA (Boise Society for Sculptural Appreciation)]]'s [[Fauxflake]] is explicitly a modified version of the Snowflake.

## Status (Oct 1997)
* The pattern likely no longer exists physically in current [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]] warehouses (presumably removed after CALLISTO). Original documentation exists in [[LTX Headquarters]] archives. Represents the first major manifestation and weaponization (albeit unintentional by Winston) of the Algorithm.
