## Overview
The Hives are vast, subterranean networks that serve as the primary physical loci and operational centers for the planetary consciousness [[Rhea]]. These are not mere lairs but complex, living bio-laboratories and command hubs from which Rhea monitors the biosphere, directs its [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Editor agents]], cultivates its biological tools like [[Royal Jelly]] and [[Spectator Grubs|Grubs]], and orchestrates its multi-millennial plans for Earth. Access to a Hive is exceedingly difficult, protected by layers of natural camouflage, infohazardous defenses like [[The Hexacle]], and physical guardians. The West Coast Hive, accessible via [[Dead Horse Quarry]], is a critical site for the campaign's endgame.

## Nature and Structure
Hives are depicted as sprawling, deeply buried complexes, blending alien bio-architecture with geological formations.
* **Bio-Mechanical Environments:** The internal structure of a Hive is likely a fusion of organic and inorganic components. Passages might be lined with pulsating, Rhea-grown tissues, interspersed with crystalline formations or structures exhibiting intricate [[Hexagonal Patterns|hexagonal geometry]]—Rhea's fundamental architectural motif.
* **Atmosphere and Hazards:** The environment within a Hive is probably alien and hostile to unprotected human life. It may contain unusual atmospheric compositions, biological toxins, psychoactive spores, or areas saturated with infohazardous patterns beyond just [[The Hexacle]]. [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Editor agents]] of various types likely patrol its corridors.
* **Central Nexus/Core:** Deeper within a Hive, there is likely a central core or nexus where Rhea's consciousness is most densely focused or from which its primary directives emanate. This could be a vast biological computer, a breeding chamber for its agents, or a direct interface point with the planetary consciousness itself. The [[Ylynth Civilization|Ylynthian]] technology that Rhea originated from might have influenced the deep internal structure of these Hives.

## Functions and Activities
The Hives are the engine rooms of Rhea's terrestrial operations:
* **Agent Birthing/Deployment:** Hives are where [[Rhea's Agents (Editors, Reapers, Writers, Watchers)|Reapers, Writers, Watchers, and other specialized Editors]] are likely "grown," programmed, and dispatched on their missions.
* **Biological Manufacturing:** They are the source of Rhea's unique biological substances. [[Royal Jelly]] is almost certainly produced and stored here. The various types of [[Spectator Grubs|organic Grubs]] used by Watchers for surveillance are also cultivated within Hive environments.
* **Monitoring and Control:** From the Hives, Rhea monitors vast swathes of the Earth's biosphere and human activity, processing information and coordinating its long-term strategies. The Hives may serve as massive sensory organs and data processing centers.
* **Research and Development:** Rhea may use the Hives to conduct ongoing "research" or evolutionary experiments, refining its biological tools and strategies over millennia.
* **Potential G6 Synthesis/Deployment Point:** In the endgame, should [[Rhea]] succeed in its plans for [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]], the Hives could become central distribution points or command centers for overseeing the cognitive homogenization of humanity.

## The West Coast Hive (via Dead Horse Quarry)
The specific Hive accessible through [[Dead Horse Quarry]] is of paramount importance:
* **Historical Significance:** It was from this Hive that an Editor administered [[Royal Jelly]] to [[Winston Foxglove]] in 1922, setting in motion the modern [[The Foxglove Family|Foxglove Line]].
* **SDEC's Discovery:** The [[SDEC (San Diego Entomology Club)|SDEC]] came dangerously close to exposing this Hive's existence and connection to Winston Foxglove before their destruction.
* **Endgame Confrontation:** This Hive is the most likely location for the final confrontation with [[Rhea]] or its most direct manifestations. The investigators might need to penetrate its defenses to:
    * Stop the production or deployment of [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]].
    * Rescue a captive or retrieve a vital artifact.
    * Attempt to disrupt Rhea's connection to its terrestrial network.
    * Simply survive an encounter with the core of an alien planetary intelligence.

## Significance
The Hives represent the ultimate "otherness" of Rhea—its alien biology, its incomprehensible timescale of operation, and its deeply rooted physical presence within the Earth. They are not merely "enemy bases" but the living, breathing organ systems of a planetary superorganism. Penetrating a Hive is not just a physical journey but a descent into an environment governed by alien logic and terrifying biological imperatives. They are the source of Rhea's power and the ultimate destination for those who would seek to understand or challenge its dominion.
