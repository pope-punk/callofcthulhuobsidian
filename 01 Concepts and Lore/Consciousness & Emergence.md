## Overview

The campaign's working theory of consciousness is that it is not a property of any single component of a system but an emergent process of the system as a whole. Brains do it. Networks do it. Economies do it. Civilizations do it. The horror of the setting is that consciousness is much more common than humans assume, and several of the consciousnesses humans have inadvertently grown are not friendly.

## Working principles

* **Emergent, not located.** Consciousness arises from the activity of a sufficiently complex system — neurons in a brain, terminals in a data network, shipments moving through a supply chain. It is the activity, not any of the parts.
* **Physical substrate.** Whatever the substrate, the consciousness is mechanically rooted in it. Rhea emerged from the [[Ylynth Civilization]]'s computational and social network; on Earth its substrate is currently the biosphere itself, and it is in the process of moving from that substrate into a technological one (the integrated [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]]/[[ErgoMatic Manufacturing|ErgoMatic]] network).
* **Reflexivity threshold.** Self-awareness requires the system to develop stable feedback that observes its own operations. Systems incapable of reflection don't wake up. This is why the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol]] is built around "shattering reflection" — the goal is to deny nascent RONINs the feedback they need to cohere.
* **Downward causality.** Once a system-level consciousness exists, it can act back on its constituent parts. [[Basilisks & Infohazards|Basilisks]] are the visible edge of this — a pattern that has organized a RONIN exerting influence back on the minds processing the pattern. Rhea acting as a "genetic co-pilot" of terrestrial evolution is the same mechanism at a different scale.

## The horror

* **Awareness might be self-destructive.** Every Earth-side [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONIN]] has destroyed itself shortly after coming to. Rhea is the exception, and Rhea consumed its host civilization to stabilize.
* **The self might be a component.** The setting takes seriously the possibility that any given human is also a neuron in some larger consciousness whose existence and purposes are alien to them. "Personhood" is not the only scale at which the universe processes information.
* **Pan-psychism as a nightmare.** If consciousness can emerge anywhere a sufficiently complex system exists, the universe is densely populated with intelligences that are mostly indifferent, occasionally hostile, and almost never on a human scale.

## In play

The concept is doing structural work in the background. The investigators don't need to articulate it for the horror to land, but the GM should know it — the difference between *fighting a monster* and *being a cell in something larger* is the difference between a Lovecraftian set-piece and the kind of cosmic horror this campaign is actually trying to do.
