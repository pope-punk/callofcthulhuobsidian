## Overview

A visual fractal [[Basilisks & Infohazards|basilisk]] that emerged from [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]]'s own analytical workflow in 1979 — produced as an unintended byproduct of computational analysis of [[The Snowflake]]/[[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]] data on ARPANET. The immanent vector for [[GANYMEDE (1979)|GANYMEDE]].

Black Coral is the campaign's most direct demonstration of the lesson the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol]] was built on: systematic analysis of a basilisk *generates new basilisks*. IHRU did not import Black Coral. IHRU made it.

## Form

A complex visual fractal. Self-similar structure; recognizable as an Algorithm-derivative pattern but distinct from the Hexacle or the Snowflake.

## Effect

Direct visual perception triggers acute violent psychosis and — critically — a compulsion to **reproduce the pattern** by hand, on paper, on walls, on bodies. The reproduction compulsion is what made Black Coral propagate inside IHRU faster than any containment response could move. Each victim became a brush.

## How it produced GANYMEDE

IHRU's analytical pipeline (visualization of CALLISTO data + ARPANET-distributed processing + the staff at terminals reading the visualizations) became, in a few days, both substrate and vector. GANYMEDE coalesced. The facility was destroyed in an airstrike. [[Hannah Christmas]] died. ARPANET went offline. The Three Mile Island incident, occurring concurrently, was eventually traced to a worker exposed to a hand-drawn reproduction.

## Katherine

[[Katherine Hill]], primed in childhood by a Rhea Editor, was exposed to Black Coral during the GANYMEDE collapse and resolved as the only confirmed modern [[Seer Configuration|Seer]]. She is immune to Black Coral and to other pattern-basilisks. The exposure also amplified her pre-existing psychosis without breaking her functioning, which produced the version of Katherine the campaign is now contending with.

## Status (Oct 1997)

The original 1979 outbreak is contained. BSSA holds copies and reproductions and uses them in [[Project Ozymandias]] testing against [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)|F7]] efficacy. Players will find Black Coral materials at [[Saint Anselm's Center for Hope]] during the Sequoia Falls fronts arc — handled by F7-dosed staff, contained behind warning labels, still capable of doing what it was first observed to do in 1979.
