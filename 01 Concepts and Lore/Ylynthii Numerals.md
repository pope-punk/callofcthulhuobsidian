## Overview
The base-six numerical system used by the extinct [[Ylynth Civilization]], based on [[Hexagonal Patterns|hexagonal computational principles]]. Understanding this system is necessary to solve the [[Safe Puzzle]]. The Foxglove family has an unconscious knowledge of this numeral system, and find it unnaturally easy to quickly handle complex computations in base-six. 
## History
[[Winston Foxglove|Winston]] was the first of the Foxglove family (though not the first humans — see the Tedesco family below) to use the numeral system. The 1928 death of his mother, [[Henrietta Foxglove|Henrietta]], activated his royal-jelly-primed [[Scions|Scion]] configuration, and Winston quickly stepped into her managerial role at Foxglove Containers and Transport, his preternatural mathematical ability perfectly suiting him to help his shattered father [[Thurston Foxglove|Thurston]] run the company. It was at this time that he began working extensively with the numerals, which eventually seemed to lead him to the [[The Foxglove Algorithm|Algorithm]] within himself. 

Before long, Foxglove Containers and Transport was operating at unheard-of levels of efficiency, utilizing highly complex track-switching and batch-processing control systems that can retrospectively be seen to anticipate some of the architecture of stack-based programming systems. The original calculations and design of these systems would eventually be confiscated by the FBI, transferred to the [[IHRU (InfoHazard Response Unit)|IHRU]], and inherited by SDEC. 

While working through the ideas that would become [[ErgoMatic Manufacturing|ErgoMatic]], Winston attempted to teach the numerical system to his son [[Oliver Foxglove|Oliver]] at a young age. Oliver never showed any particular knack for using the mathematical system until his mother, [[Andrea Foxglove]], passed tragically. This loss activated his latent genetic proficiency. However, the connection between maternal loss and mathematical talent went unnoticed, since Winston had already taught Oliver the system directly. 

Oliver made frequent use of the system while in his father's employ at ErgoMatic, and became a significant driver of the company's technological direction in his own right. While his father was committed to prosthetics manufacture (partially motivated by a desire to pay back an imagined debt to the many victims of [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]] who had lost limbs in the explosion), Oliver hammered away at developing a semi-autonomous biotechnics division — convinced, for reasons he assumed were his own, that limb regrowth was the future, not limb replacement. Many of his contributions were calculated using Ylynthii numerals, but versions in base 10 were produced for use by the company's engineers and scientists.  

While Winston shared his gift with his son, Oliver was far more circumspect. He grew up having seen the damage that his father's gift had wrought, and his father's lifelong contacts with IHRU and later SDEC agents gave him an acute sense of the danger wrapped up in the Algorithm. Oliver, on some level, sought reproachment and reconciliation with the world which had so harshly judged his father. Therefore, [[Syd Foxglove|Syd's]] spontaneous adoption of the numeral system came as an alarming surprise to Oliver when he discovered it in 1997.

## System
Notation used below: `S(n)` denotes the Ylynthii glyph for digit `n`. Comma-separated tuples like `S(2,3,2,4,2)` denote a multi-digit Ylynthii numeral, read left-to-right with the most significant digit first. "Covered" indicates which digits are obscured or partially destroyed in Syd's actual carving (i.e., what the players will *not* be able to read directly).

## Significance
* Direct link to the alien origin of [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] and [[Rhea]].
* Required component for converting [[Andrea Foxglove]]'s Base-10 birthday into the correct format for [[Oliver Foxglove]]'s cipher as part of the [[Safe Puzzle]].
* Appeared in the works of **Giovanni Tedesco**, a Renaissance-era Venetian architect, and survive in transcriptions made by his daughter **Giulia Tedesco**, a nun.
	* Giovanni designed an entire stretch of Venice's waterfront district during the city's mercantile peak, integrating shipyards and cultural institutions into a layer of tightly-wound hex-spiral streets. What appears to have been an early basilisk propagated through Europe as a dancing plague — a feedback loop between multiple dancers whose foundational movements were structurally produced by the architecture of the district itself. The extent to which this nascent RONIN ever parasitized the broader econosphere — versus remaining a highly local emergence sustained by the Venetian street pattern itself — is unclear. The latter possibility appears to have informed [[Katherine Hill|Katherine's]] approach to [[IO (Emerging 1997)|Io]].
	* Giovanni was burned at the stake for heresy. Giulia was sent to a convent, ostensibly to be saved from her father's influence. No copies of Giovanni's original architectural plans survive intact, but Ylynthii numerals remain in the palimpsests of the Bible pages Giulia transcribed during her remaining years.
	* Partial records were located by the IHRU, but the Ylynthii script was never positively identified as matching Winston's notes. By the time Katherine obtained copies of Giulia's transcriptions, Winston's notes had been transferred to SDEC. 
* **[[Katherine Hill]] Recognition:** Katherine Hill, through research into the Tedesco family  infohazards/anomalies, **recognized the symbols** produced by Syd in captivity. This dramatically increased Syd's perceived importance to her, as apparently connected to historical proto-RONIN events.
