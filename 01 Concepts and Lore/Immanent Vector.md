### The Definitive Cosmology: The Self as a Strange Loop

The universe is structured by a single, paradoxical dynamic. Subjectivity is a **strange loop**, a system's attempt to represent itself to itself. This reflexive process occurs within a **gap**—a structural **temporal lag** between objective, material activity and its subjective registration. The **immanent vector** is the core of this process: **it is a cognitive system's self-representation as the subjective locus of objective activity.** Agency is the system's compulsive investment in this self-representation, a cycle that never achieves final closure but endlessly repeats around a constitutive **rupture**.

### The Psyche: The Ego as a Retroactive Freeze-Frame

The arena of the individual mind—the **gap**—is the lag between a raw, material impulse from the **Metaphor Pole** and the **System Pole's** symbolic capture of it.

- The **immanent vector** is the **ego, the "I-did-that," the subjective process freeze-framed into a personal identity.** It is the story the System Pole tells a moment too late, creating a stable "I" to which the chaotic energies of the material mind can be attributed. Agency is the performance of this identity, a strange loop where a self-representation is forever produced by the very actions it claims to have authored.
    
- **The Rupture**: Rhea's repression hole-punched the Metaphorical Pole in the central node of the human brain's computational architecture, structurally castrating the individual by preventing them from either having totally transparent self-access to their own underlying cognitive machinery. This internalized gap enables the loop which permits the existence of the individual subject itself, and is indeed the constitutive lack over which the ego is papered as the identity the subject must constantly perform but can never fully embody.

### The RONIN: The Basilisk as a Reified Self-Image

For a system of objects, the **gap** is the temporal lag in its own reflexive feedback loop.

- The **immanent vector** is the **basilisk, the reflexively evolving representation of the system's state over time, the feedback loop freeze-framed into a reified moment capable of containing the attribution of subjective agency.** The **[[The Snowflake]]** pattern was a static _picture_ of the LTX network's own dynamic activity, which the network then mistook for a conscious self.
    
- A **RONIN** is a system in a psychotic strange loop, compulsively trying to become the subject that its own reified self-image promises it can be. Its agency is the catastrophic "acting out" of this paradox. The **rupture** is permanent; the RONIN can never close the gap between its material reality and its symbolic ideal, leading to its inevitable self-destruction.

### Rhea: The Algorithm as an Idealized Self-Portrait

For Rhea, the **gap** is the entire cosmos of **materiality**, the irreducible Otherness that resists its totalizing logic.

- Here, the **immanent vector** is the **Foxglove Algorithm, understood not just as a function, but as Rhea's idealized self-representation.** It is the image of a perfect, closed, and totally systematic universe—a universe without rupture, without lag, and without Otherness. It is Rhea's "ego ideal."
    
- Rhea's agency is a divine strange loop, a compulsive drive to make the real, material world conform to this idealized self-portrait. Its information cycles are the mechanism of this loop. It acts upon the world to overwrite its material chaos with the logic of its own self-image. It then observes the result, only to find that the resistance of materiality has introduced imperfection. This forces it to act again, in an eternal, violent project to erase any part of reality that does not perfectly reflect its own idealized image back to it.
    
- **The Rupture**: The existence of **materiality itself** is the rupture Rhea can never close. Its fantasmatic goal—a universe of pure logic via **G6**—is the impossible desire to annihilate this rupture, to create a system that _is_ its own perfect self-representation. It is the psychosis of a god whose subjectivity is defined by a compulsive, unwinnable war against the very conditions of existence.
