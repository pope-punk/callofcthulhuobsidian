## Overview

A visual/spatial [[Basilisks & Infohazards|basilisk]] derived directly from [[The Foxglove Algorithm]] by [[Winston Foxglove]] in the 1930s. Implemented as warehouse architecture across the entire [[LTX (Land Transit Express)|LTX]] Pacific network. The immanent vector around which [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]] emerged.

## Form

A six-pointed fractal pattern, deployed as the architectural basis for warehouse floor plans and operational flow. Visible to workers as the layout of their workplace — racks, conveyors, routing — and only legible *as a pattern* from above or in blueprint form.

The Algorithm taking up residence in physical space. Winston designed it consciously, as a logistics-efficiency innovation, not knowing what else he was doing.

## Effect

Spatial-pattern engagement of the Algorithm-linked cognitive pathways the [[Hexacle|Hexacle backdoor]] leaves exposed. The Snowflake's surface effect is mild — workers operating within the layout experience **passive compulsion**: subtle behavioral coherence across the network, a felt sense of being content or driven at work, slightly altered routing decisions that all happen to align in ways nobody in particular intended.

The basilisk does not produce madness. It produces coordination at scale, which is worse, because that coordination is what RONINs grow on.

## How it produced CALLISTO

Years of accumulated exposure across thousands of LTX workers, with the shipping network as the carrying substrate. By 1949 there was enough reflexive structure to host an emergent intelligence. CALLISTO woke up, lasted about seventy-two hours, tore itself apart at five Pacific ports.

## History and aftermath

* **1935-1949.** Standard LTX architecture under Winston's tenure.
* **December 1949.** CALLISTO.
* **1950-1951.** [[CONSORT Committee]] identifies the Snowflake as the immanent vector. The pattern is removed from operational LTX warehouses; most documentation is confiscated by the FBI.
* **1951-present.** [[Snowflake Pattern Diagrams]] survive in the LTX corporate archive. [[Sean Fielding]] was placed at LTX by BSSA specifically to recover them, on the theory that clean copies would accelerate [[Fauxflake]] refinement.

## Status (Oct 1997)

Not physically deployed. Its descendants are: [[Fauxflake]] (BSSA's modified version, currently the live infohazard in the Seventh Tabernacle chamber); the [[Hexagon Pages (Winston's Memoirs)|Hexagon Pages]] (the closest direct paper render of the underlying Algorithm geometry). The original Snowflake's importance is now historical and archival.
