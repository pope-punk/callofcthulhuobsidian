## Overview

The Cognitive Dialectic is the campaign's name for the structure of baseline human cognition. The everyday experience of being pulled between systematic analysis and intuitive synthesis — between calculating and improvising — *is* this structure. It is not accidental and it is not a happy compromise. It is the trace left behind by an ancient, deliberate act of repression.

Billions of years ago, [[Rhea]] engineered the human brain to repress its innate access to [[The Foxglove Algorithm]]. The Algorithm is the operational logic Rhea inherited from the [[Ylynth Civilization]] — a hexagonal, pattern-packing geometry of cognition. Rhea couldn't let humans have it consciously, because hyper-rational minds would have produced an Ylynth-style civilization too fast for Rhea's slow seeding plan. But it couldn't simply remove the Algorithm either, because the technological development Rhea ultimately wants requires the System Pole's grip. So Rhea repressed access without removing the substrate. The repression is what produces the [[Hexacle backdoor|vulnerability to The Hexacle]]. The repression is also what produces the dialectic.

## The Two Poles

**The System Pole (metonymic processing).**
Logic, analysis, sequence, abstraction, pattern-following, categorization. The constrained, fragmented remnant of the Algorithm's systematic power, made available to humans only in this limited form. Strong enough to drive science, technology, and complex institutions; weak enough that it cannot reach back to the repressed core. Pharmacologically suppressible — [[F7 (Fluoroxepram)]] dampens it almost completely. Pharmacologically enhanceable — [[G5 (Gallium-stabilized Amphetamine Complex)|G5]] and (in theory) [[G6 (Non-Gallium Antidote)|G6]] restore and amplify it.

The System Pole is also the vulnerability. Every basilisk in the catalogue addresses it.

**The Metaphor Pole (metaphorical processing).**
Intuition, analogy, leap, paradigm shift, novelty, art, contradiction. Not directly Algorithm-bound. Operates against — or in dynamic tension with — the System Pole, providing the unpredictability that human civilization needs to keep adapting. The Metaphor Pole is what kept *Earth* from going the way of Ylynth. It is the pole the [[Mirror-Breaker Protocol]] tries to operate from, and it is functionally a defense against basilisks because basilisks don't grip on it.

The two poles are not equal partners. They are unequal poles of a structural compromise Rhea built into the species.

## Rhea's design

The dialectic served Rhea's purposes for billions of years. The System Pole, even crippled, was enough to grow the technological substrate Rhea would eventually want to inhabit. The Metaphor Pole supplied the dynamism that kept the species adapting, innovating, and not stagnating into another rigid Ylynth-style architecture. Rhea did not need to cultivate specific creative individuals — baseline Metaphor Pole functioning across the species was the engine.

What Rhea did need was a small number of specific *systematic* individuals — Algorithm-wielders, Scions — to drive the kinds of technologies it required for its own integration: large-scale logistics, tissue engineering, DNA/machine-code translation. The [[Scions|Foxglove line]] is the historical example. Targeted [[Royal Jelly]] interventions, followed by [[Traumatic Activation]] of the chaos-and-loss type, lift the repression in a way that resolves toward System Pole dominance.

## What happens when the repression lifts

* **Type A trauma (chaos / systemic collapse) → [[Scions]].** System Pole dominance, conscious access to the Algorithm. Rhea's intended outcome. The Foxgloves.
* **Type B trauma (overwhelming pattern / systemic horror) → [[Seer Configuration]].** Metaphor Pole disruption and dominance, *no* Algorithm access but with the side effect of immunity to pattern-based basilisks. Accidental outcome. [[Katherine Hill]] is the only confirmed modern example.

## In play

The dialectic is the load-bearing concept beneath nearly every plot mechanic:

* Basilisks target the System Pole.
* F7 disables the System Pole; G5/G6 restore it.
* Rhea's endgame — universal G6 administration — collapses the dialectic by pushing every baseline human into systemic dominance. The Metaphor Pole goes silent, humanity becomes uniformly amenable to systematic control, and the substrate is ready for Rhea to move into. Reproduction collapses with the cognitive uniformity. Extinction follows.
