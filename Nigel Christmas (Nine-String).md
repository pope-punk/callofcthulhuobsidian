## Overview

Nigel "Nine-String" Christmas (b. 1932). Director of [[BNJC (Blue Note Jazz Collective)|BNJC]]'s Sequoia Falls cell. Son of FBI Assistant Director [[Andrew Christmas]] (killed in [[CALLISTO (1949)|CALLISTO]]) and IHRU founder/director [[Hannah Christmas]] (killed in [[GANYMEDE (1979)|GANYMEDE]]).

The campaign's principal institutional ally for the investigators. Also, by inclination and by long habit, a spymaster — he tells the truth selectively, compartmentalizes from his own people, and tests the loyalty of new contacts before he gives them anything load-bearing.

## Background

Nigel's family has been the institutional core of the American infohazard response apparatus for three generations. His father died in the event that made [[RONINs (Randomly Organizing Neural Information Networks)|RONINs]] a recognized phenomenon; his mother founded the agency that died trying to study them; he leads one of the seven cells that came out of his mother's agency. He understands the Mirror-Breaker theory intimately and has watched it lose ground to BSSA's interventionist drift for two decades. He is sixty-five years old in 1997 and tired in ways that are specific.

He took over BNJC's Sequoia Falls operation in the early 1990s after the relocation from Sacramento. He sponsored [[Alice Redmond (Agent Altissimo)|Alice's]] EUROPA fieldwork, then the off-books continuation under Blanc, then the Dylan Hill surveillance, then the covert support for Alice's rogue operation against the Signal Dummies. He has been making increasingly aggressive compartmentalization decisions across the same period, which is how Alice ended up with an entire PCBOT-bypass file that BNJC officially does not know exists.

## What he knows about Montreal

Nigel is the only BNJC officer who knows that **Blanc was Alice's longtime EUROPA-era contact** and that Alice sent Syd to him in July 1997. He does **not** know:

* Blanc's apartment address. Alice never gave it to him. Their channel ran through Alice exclusively.
* That Blanc and Alice assembled the encrypted PCBOT-bypass methodology and stored it on Blanc's drive.
* That Blanc has been captured (not killed, not merely silent).

The burner number to Syd is dead. Chrysalis (his proxy to Alice) has not reported in since COTU. He has lost most of his operational visibility into Alice's situation simultaneously.

## What he'll do when the players bring back the Montreal number

Confirm that Blanc was Alice's primary Montreal contact. Admit he has no current address or working channel. Suggest — perhaps obliquely — that the operational path to Blanc has to come through someone Alice had compartmentalized away from BNJC, which means [[Maria Chen]] via the Brewster lockbox lead. Brief the players on [[SDEC Facility|SDEC]], the Ant Trap, and Chrysalis's role as the last surviving SDEC field agent — and note that Chrysalis is now missing. Task the players with finding Alice (cure and intelligence both). Arrange [[UMSE (Upper Michigan Succulent Enthusiasts)|UMSE]] field support for Montreal once they have an address.

Almost all of this conversation will be at least partly overheard by [[Steven Rober (Agent Shutter)|Agent Shutter]], whom Nigel introduced to the cell two weeks earlier and has not yet realized is a mole.

## In play

Nine-String is a useful ally with significant compromised intelligence and a tendency to give the players just enough to act on without ever giving them the whole picture at once. He is honest in his judgments and selective in his disclosures. His operational doctrine is that anyone who needs to know more than he is giving them should be capable of figuring it out — which is, on a good day, the Mirror-Breaker style of mentorship, and on a bad day a structural failure mode that costs lives.

His personal containment protocol — used if BNJC's Sequoia Falls cell is compromised beyond recovery — is "The Silent Coda," triggered by a saxophone key.
